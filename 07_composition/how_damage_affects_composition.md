# HOW DAMAGE AFFECTS COMPOSITIONThis file documents the ways that damage alters visual composition—focal hierarchy, balance, reading order, and spatial organization.## DefinitionDamage composition refers to the systematic effects of artifacts on how viewers perceive and navigate image structure.## Damage as compositional element### Frame division- Scanlines create horizontal rhythm- Banding creates horizontal zones- Tearing creates fractured planes- Blocks create grid structure### Focal competition- Bright damage competes with subject- High-contrast artifacts draw eye- Motion in damage distracts from subject motion- Texture in damage competes with subject texture### Edge effects- Damage at edges frames or distracts- Vignetting from damage focuses or obscures- Border artifacts create container or intrusion### Depth disruption- Haze/flare flattens space- Noise reduces depth cues- Sharp damage artifacts contradict atmospheric depth## Compositional damage types### ObscurationalDamage covers or hides compositional elements.- Scratches across faces- Blobs over text- Banding across important forms### DisruptiveDamage breaks expected visual flow.- Tearing at reading path- Noise in quiet areas- Contrast inversion### StructuralDamage adds new compositional structure.- Scanline grid- Block pattern- Symmetrical flare### TransformativeDamage changes the nature of the image.- Abstraction through severe artifacting- Mood shift through color damage- Temporal confusion through motion artifacts## Damage severity and compositionS1–S2: Texture, atmosphere—compositional enhancement possibleS3: Active participant—must be composed withS4–S5: Dominant or destructive—composition becomes about damage itself## Strategic damage composition### EmphasisUse damage to draw attention:- Light leak on subject- Bloom around focal point- Grain texture on important surface### ObscurationUse damage to hide or soften:- Haze over background- Noise in shadows- Smear over distraction### RhythmUse damage pattern for structure:- Scanlines for horizontal rhythm- Banding for zoning- Dust for random texture### MoodUse damage for emotional affect:- Warm decay for nostalgia- Digital sickliness for anxiety- Severe damage for tragedy or horror## Damage composition risks### Unintentional focal competitionDamage draws eye away from subject.### Unintentional obscurationImportant information lost to artifact.### Chaotic hierarchyMultiple damage types competing without structure.### Legibility collapseDamage destroys ability to read image at all.## Prompting compositional damageConsider placement and severity:> "light leak framing subject from left edge, moderate grain adding texture to background, subtle haze reducing background competition"## SummaryDamage is not just surface texture—it is compositional force, reshaping how images are read and felt.
