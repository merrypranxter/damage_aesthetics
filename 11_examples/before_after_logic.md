# BEFORE/AFTER LOGICThis file documents how to construct and interpret before/after comparisons in damage analysis and generation.## DefinitionBefore/after logic refers to the systematic comparison of undamaged and damaged states to understand, demonstrate, and control damage effects.## Before/after purposes### EducationalDemonstrate what specific artifacts look like in isolation.### ForensicIdentify what damage has occurred to a known original.### GenerativeControl the transformation from clean to damaged.### ValidationVerify that damage systems produce intended results.## Before/after construction### The clean baselineRequirements:- Same content as damaged version- Same basic composition and lighting- No damage artifacts (S0)- Neutral or documented processing### The damaged versionRequirements:- Identical content to baseline- Specific, documented damage stack- Controlled severity progression- Isolated variables (one artifact type changing, or full stack)### Comparison methods**Side-by-side:** Clean | Damaged- Best for: Immediate visual comparison- Risk: Viewer's eye may miss subtle changes**Wipe/slider:** Interactive reveal- Best for: Detailed inspection of specific regions- Risk: Technical implementation required**Difference map:** Visualized change only- Best for: Technical analysis, artifact isolation- Risk: Abstract, not perceptually intuitive**Temporal fade:** Clean → Damaged over time- Best for: Video, severity progression- Risk: May miss static details## Severity progression seriesConstruct multiple afters showing S1→S2→S3→S4→S5:| Stage | Description | Use ||-------|-------------|-----|| S0 | Clean baseline | Reference || S1-S2 | Trace to mild | Subtle atmosphere || S3 | Moderate | Stylistic active || S4 | Severe | Dramatic, forensic || S5 | Catastrophic | Abstract, horror |## Before/after for specific artifacts### Single artifact isolationShow:- Clean- Artifact only (S3)- Artifact in stack contextExample: Chroma bleed alone, then chroma bleed with native VHS stack.### Stack accumulationShow:- Clean- + Layer 1- + Layer 2- + Layer 3- Final stackDemonstrates how damage builds and interacts.### Chain progressionShow:- Original (film)- + VHS transfer- + CRT playback- + Phone capture- + Platform uploadDemonstrates generational accumulation.## Forensic before/afterWhen original is unknown:### Reverse engineering1. Analyze damaged image for artifacts2. Infer medium and cause3. Reconstruct plausible original4. Validate: does damage stack produce observed result?### Uncertainty documentationAcknowledge when multiple originals could produce same damage:- "Could be film → VHS or early digital → VHS"- "Light leak could be camera or scanner"## Generative before/after control### Prompt-based transformation
Before: “Clean digital photograph, neutral processing”
After: “Same photograph with VHS transfer: chroma bleed S3, luma softness S3, tracking instability S2, played on CRT with phosphor bloom S2”
### Parameter-based transformation| Parameter | Before | After ||-----------|--------|-------|| grain_intensity | 0.0 | 0.6 || chroma_bleed | 0.0 | 0.5 || tracking_error | 0.0 | 0.3 || bloom_threshold | 1.0 | 0.7 |## Validation checklistBefore/after pairs should demonstrate:- [ ] Artifact is visible and identifiable- [ ] Severity matches specification- [ ] Medium behavior is correct- [ ] No unintended artifacts introduced- [ ] Content remains recognizable (unless S5)- [ ] Affective quality matches intent## Common before/after failures### Uncontrolled variablesDifferent content, lighting, or composition between before/after.### Severity inflationS3 specified but S5 delivered (or vice versa).### Artifact confusionDifferent damage than specified appears.### Content destructionEven S5 should show transformation, not total replacement.## SummaryBefore/after logic is the scientific method of damage—controlled comparison revealing the specific effects of specific failures on specific images.
