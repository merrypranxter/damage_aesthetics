# CONTAMINATION STACKSThis file documents specific combinations of surface contamination artifacts—how multiple contaminants interact and layer.## DefinitionContamination stacks are combinations of surface contamination artifacts (dust, scratches, oils, moisture, etc.) that co-occur in real or stylized damage scenarios.## The Dirty Lens Stack### Components1. **Fingerprint oils** — crescent patterns, contrast loss2. **Dust particles** — scattered soft spots3. **Moisture film** — broad haze, highlight bloom4. **Grease residue** — veiling glare, smear### Interaction- Oils create base haze and flare- Dust adds discrete interruption- Moisture softens everything further- Grease emphasizes highlight spread### Severity progressionS2: light fingerprintingS3: obvious multi-contaminationS4: heavy haze, major readability impact---## The Neglected Film Stack### Components1. **Emulsion scratches** — vertical or transport-aligned2. **Dust accumulation** — random flecks3. **Hair/fiber intrusion** — curved strands4. **Chemical stain** — color shift, patchy5. **Mold spots** — organic patterning### Interaction- Scratches provide linear structure- Dust and hair add particulate chaos- Chemical and mold add color and organic texture- Combined: archaeological neglect### Severity progressionS2–S3: archive conditionS4–S5: archaeological excavation, near-ruin---## The Multi-Generation Print Stack### Components1. **Toner drag** — directional streaking2. **Misregistration** — color fringing3. **Copy decay** — contrast flattening4. **Scanner bands** — regular stripes5. **Dust from repeated handling** — random specks### Interaction- Each generation adds its own signature- Toner and registration = mechanical print- Scanner bands = digitization layer- Dust = handling history### Severity progressionG2–G3: obvious wearG5+: extreme abstraction, "xerox of xerox"---## The Environmental Exposure Stack### Components1. **Moisture damage** — warping, stains2. **Mold growth** — organic veining, color shift3. **Heat damage** — curling, emulsion cracking4. **Light fading** — color loss, contrast reduction5. **Pollutant staining** — discoloration, spots### Interaction- Moisture enables mold- Heat accelerates all chemical processes- Light and pollutants add color changes- Combined: total environmental assault### Severity progressionS3: clear environmental damageS5: medium destruction, information loss---## Contamination stack design### Layer by material- Organic (oils, mold)- Particulate (dust, toner)- Chemical (stains, processing)- Physical (scratches, abrasion)### Consider accumulation order- What happened first?- What enables what else?- What covers or obscures earlier damage?### Legibility thresholdContamination stacks quickly obscure content.Monitor cumulative severity.## Prompting contaminationBuild by layer:> "fingerprint-smudged lens with dust accumulation, moisture condensation, and greasy residue causing veiling flare"Each component adds specific visual behavior.## SummaryContamination stacks are the archaeology of surface interaction—each layer of matter telling part of the handling and storage history.
