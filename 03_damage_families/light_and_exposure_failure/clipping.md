# CLIPPINGThis file documents clipping as a cross-medium light/exposure failure—when brightness exceeds the recording or display system's capacity, causing information loss at the highlight extreme.## DefinitionClipping refers to the visible loss of detail in bright regions where the signal or material reaches its maximum capacity and "flattens" or "blows out" to pure white or maximum value.## Core visual identityClipping feels:- hard-edged at the boundary- detail-void in the center- brightness-maximum- system-limitation based- sometimes harsh, sometimes ethereal## Medium-specific manifestations### Digital sensor (highlight clipping)- hard cut to pure white- color channel imbalance (one channel clips first)- "magenta sunset" or color shift in clipped regions- unrecoverable in raw if fully clipped### Film (shoulder compression)- softer, more gradual compression- possible color shift in extreme overexposure- chemical/material limit### Analog video (white crush)- hard clipping in broadcast-safe limits- possible blooming or edge artifacts- signal-level based### Display (output clipping)- tone-mapping limits- HDR highlight compression- visible as "flat" bright regions## Clipping types### Hard clippingAbrupt transition to maximum value, detail completely lost.### Soft compressionGradual shoulder, some tonal variation preserved.### Channel clippingIndividual RGB channels clip at different rates, causing color shifts.## Severity rangeS1–S2: limited specular highlights, minor detail lossS3: obvious blown regions, some subject detail lostS4–S5: massive highlight takeover, severe subject destruction, abstract white fields## Stack partnersOften with:- bloom (clipping often blooms)- halation (film clipping)- overexposure- contrast expansion- aggressive grading## Confusion risks### Clipping vs bloomClipping is the detail loss; bloom is the spread around it.### Clipping vs overexposureOverexposure is the cause; clipping is the visible result.## SummaryClipping is the image hitting its brightness ceiling—light overwhelming the system's ability to record or display nuance, collapsing into pure luminous void.
