# COLOR CHANNEL MISALIGNMENTThis file documents color channel misalignment as a cross-medium damage family—when color components fail to register correctly, producing fringes, ghosts, or color shifts.## DefinitionColor channel misalignment refers to visible displacement, timing errors, or registration failures between color components (RGB, CMYK, YUV, etc.), producing colored edges, fringes, or ghost images.## Core visual identityMisalignment feels:- rainbow-fringed- edge-hugging- displaced or doubled- registration-based- mechanically or electrically wrong- often directional## Medium-specific manifestations### Analog video (chroma delay)- Y/C separation causing color fringes- horizontal color displacement- composite video limitation### Film (registration error)- CMYK or RGB separation in printing- mechanical tolerance in multi-pass systems- color fringing on edges### Digital (channel offset)- deliberate or accidental RGB displacement- algorithmic or manual effect- sharp, computational appearance### Display (convergence error)- CRT color gun misalignment- soft color fringes- geometry-dependent### Lens (chromatic aberration)- optical wavelength separation- purple/green fringes on high contrast- aperture and focal-length dependent## Misalignment types### Lateral displacementChannels shifted horizontally or vertically.### Timing/delayChannels arriving at different times (temporal misalignment).### Scale differentialChannels sized differently (geometric misregistration).### RotationChannels rotated relative to each other.## Severity rangeS1–S2: subtle fringing, minor edge colorS3: obvious displacement, clear color separationS4–S5: severe channel separation, abstract color decomposition## Stack partnersOften with:- sharp edges (makes misalignment visible)- high contrast- mechanical wear- optical limitations- composite signal degradation## Confusion risks### Misalignment vs chromatic aberrationAberration is optical; misalignment can be electronic, mechanical, or computational.### Misalignment vs chroma bleedBleed is signal leakage; misalignment is positional error.## SummaryColor channel misalignment is the divorce of color components—RGB, CMYK, or YUV failing to marry properly, leaving rainbow trails and ghosted edges as evidence of their separation.
