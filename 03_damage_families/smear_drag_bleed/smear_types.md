# SMEAR TYPESThis file documents smear, drag, and bleed as a cross-medium damage family—directional spreading or leakage of image information.## DefinitionSmear/drag/bleed refers to visible directional redistribution of image content, where information spreads, leaks, or drags beyond its original boundaries.## Core visual identitySmear feels:- directional- soft-edged- information-spreading- lagging or leaking- often horizontal in analog systems- often brightness or color-weighted## Medium-specific manifestations### Analog tape (chroma smear)- color bleeding horizontally- luma/chroma separation- bandwidth-limitation based### Optical (grease/lens smear)- highlight-dependent spread- irregular, tactile- contamination-based### Digital codec (motion smear)- prediction-based trailing- block-structured- temporal residue### Display (phosphor persistence)- brightness-weighted trail- temporal decay- screen-native## Smear subtypes### Chroma bleedColor information leaking beyond luma boundaries.### Motion dragMovement creating directional soft trails.### Tape smearAnalog signal spreading horizontally.### Optical smearContamination causing highlight spread.### Compression smearPrediction error creating temporal residue.## Common traits across media- directional (often horizontal, sometimes radial)- softens edges- spreads information beyond boundaries- implies system bandwidth or response limitation- often affects color or brightness differentially## Severity rangeS1–S2: subtle edge softening, slight dragS3: obvious spreading, directional trailsS4–S5: severe information loss, dominant smear fields## Stack partnersOften with:- bandwidth limitations- contamination- motion- prediction errors- display persistence## Confusion risks### Smear vs blurBlur is general softening; smear is directional spreading.### Smear vs ghostGhost is discrete repetition; smear is continuous spread.## SummarySmear is the image refusing to stay in its lane—information bleeding, dragging, and spreading according to the limitations and contaminations of its medium.
