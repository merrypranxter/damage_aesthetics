# HORIZONTAL TEARINGThis file documents horizontal tearing as a cross-medium damage family—discontinuities in image alignment that split or shear the image horizontally.## DefinitionHorizontal tearing refers to visible discontinuities where portions of the image appear offset, sheared, or misaligned along horizontal axes, often due to timing or synchronization failures.## Core visual identityTearing feels:- mechanically wrong- horizontally fractured- timing-based- often rhythmic or periodic- like the image is slipping its gears## Medium-specific manifestations### Analog video (tracking tear)- horizontal bands offset from main image- rolling or periodic- tape/head misalignment### Digital display (screen tearing)- frame buffer mismatch with refresh- discrete horizontal splits- gaming or high-motion contexts### Broadcast (sync tear)- signal timing failure- horizontal displacement- reception-related### Digital corruption (decode tear)- block misalignment- packet-loss displacement- compression-related## Common traits across media- horizontal orientation (usually)- displacement or shear- timing/synchronization related- often affects motion or playback- implies mechanical or signal failure## Severity rangeS1–S2: subtle jitter, minor line displacementS3: obvious tearing, clear horizontal fracturesS4–S5: severe image breakup, unreadable displacement## Stack partnersOften with:- sync loss- tracking errors- timing instability- mechanical wear- signal weakness## Confusion risks### Tearing vs rollingTearing is discrete displacement; rolling is continuous movement.### Tearing vs glitch displacementTearing is timing/scan-based; glitch is often computational.## SummaryHorizontal tearing is the image's horizontal integrity failing—scan lines, frames, or blocks slipping past each other in time and space.
