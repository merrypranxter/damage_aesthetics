# MACROBLOCKSThis file documents macroblocking as the signature artifact of block-based digital compression—visible segmentation of the image into rectangular regions.## DefinitionMacroblocking refers to the visible division of compressed images into block-shaped regions (typically 8×8, 16×16, or similar) due to discrete cosine transform (DCT) coding and coarse quantization.## Core visual identityMacroblocks feel:- grid-like- computational- region-based- edge-defined- compression-native- often temporally unstable## Visible traits- visible square or rectangular boundaries- texture simplification within blocks- discontinuities at block edges- temporal flicker or shift of block boundaries- worse in motion, gradients, and low-bitrate regions## Medium/context manifestations### JPEG still images- static block artifacts in high-compression saves- gradient banding between blocks- mosquito noise at block edges### H.264/H.265 video- motion-compensated block fields- interframe block persistence- variable block sizes (4×4 to 16×16 or larger)### Streaming video- bitrate-starvation blockiness- buffer-related quality drops- adaptive streaming block transitions## Causes- coarse quantization- insufficient bits for complex regions- motion prediction errors- DCT coefficient truncation- aggressive compression profiles## Severity rangeS1–S2: subtle block edges in difficult regionsS3: obvious block structure, clear segmentationS4–S5: dominant grid, severe texture loss, block animation overwhelming content## Stack partnersOften with:- mosquito noise- banding- temporal smear- edge ringing- prediction errors## Confusion risks### Macroblocks vs pixelationPixelation is uniform low resolution; macroblocks are compression-specific artifacts at any resolution.### Macroblocks vs mosaicMosaic is deliberate censorship; macroblocks are compression scars.## SummaryMacroblocks are the visible grid of DCT compression—the image forced into mathematical tiles, each approximated rather than truly represented.
