# AFTERIMAGESThis file documents afterimages as a cross-medium temporal memory artifact—visual persistence of previous frames or bright elements across time.## DefinitionAfterimages refer to visible persistence or ghosting of visual information from previous moments, creating layered, trailing, or repeating temporal effects.## Core visual identityAfterimages feel:- temporal (time-based)- ghostly or layered- persistence-based- often brightness-weighted- sometimes rhythmic or decaying- memory-like## Medium-specific manifestations### Display (phosphor persistence)- CRT phosphor decay trails- brightness-weighted- exponential fade- screen-native### Vision (physiological afterimage)- retinal persistence- complementary colors- motion-induced blur- biological basis### Photography (long exposure)- temporal accumulation on single frame- motion trails- time compressed to space### Video (frame echo)- deliberate or accidental frame blending- ghost frames- opacity-based layering### Digital (feedback systems)- video feedback loops- temporal recursion- deliberate echo effects## Afterimage types### Positive afterimageSame polarity as original (bright stays bright).### Negative afterimageInverted polarity (complementary colors, dark becomes light).### Motion trailSpatial streak from moving bright objects.### Frame ghostDiscrete previous frame visible overlaid.## Severity rangeS1–S2: subtle trails, faint persistenceS3: obvious afterimages, clear temporal layeringS4–S5: dominant persistence, severe temporal confusion, strobe-like layering## Stack partnersOften with:- bright sources- motion- display characteristics- feedback systems- long exposure- strobe lighting## Confusion risks### Afterimage vs motion blurBlur is continuous integration; afterimage is discrete persistence.### Afterimage vs ghost framesGhost frames are full-frame; afterimages can be localized and brightness-dependent.## SummaryAfterimages are time made visible—previous moments refusing to disappear, layering themselves onto the present, creating ghostly palimpsests of visual memory.
