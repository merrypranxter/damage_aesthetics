# STATIC NOISEThis file documents static noise as a cross-medium damage family—distributed, random or pseudo-random signal interference that appears across the image field.## DefinitionStatic noise refers to visible, spatially distributed signal variation that introduces texture, speckle, or interference patterns without coherent structure.## Core visual identityStatic feels:- random or pseudo-random- field-like (spread across image)- electrically or materially generated- texture-adding- often high-frequency- unstable or crawling in motion## Medium-specific manifestations### Analog video (snow)- black and white or desaturated speckle- unstable, dancing field- signal-weakness based- horizontally biased in some systems### Digital sensor (noise)- luminance or chroma speckle- shadow-weighted- ISO/gain dependent- pixel-tethered### Film (grain)- particulate, organic- stock-dependent- tonally embedded- materially native### Compression (chatter/mosquito)- edge-clustered- block-adjacent- quantization-based- computationally patterned## Common traits across media- distributed rather than localized- adds texture to uniform areas- reduces perceived cleanliness- implies system struggle or material limitation- often more visible in shadows or low-signal regions## Severity rangeS1: subtle texture, atmosphericS2–S3: clear noise field, texture-definingS4–S5: dominant interference, readability threatened## Stack partnersOften with:- signal weakness- low light / high gain- compression stress- analog transmission issues## Confusion risks### Static vs patternStatic is random; pattern has structure.### Static vs grainRelated but medium-specific—preserve the distinction.## SummaryStatic noise is the visual sound of systems struggling against their own limitations—electrical, material, or computational hiss made visible.
