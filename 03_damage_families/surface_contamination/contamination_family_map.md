# CONTAMINATION FAMILY MAPThis file maps the complete surface contamination family across all media contexts—matter interfering with optical or mechanical image paths.## Core behaviorPhysical matter introducing visible artifacts through obstruction, scattering, or abrasion.## Family members### Dust- **Scale:** microscopic to visible flecks- **Location:** lens, film, sensor, scanner, projection path- **Character:** discrete spots, sharp or soft- **Motion:** static or dancing### Scratches- **Scale:** fine lines to wide abrasions- **Location:** film emulsion, display surfaces, optics- **Character:** linear, often directional- **Motion:** frame-locked or transport-aligned### Hair / fiber / lint- **Scale:** strand-like, curved or straight- **Location:** gate, scanner, lens near-field- **Character:** soft or sharp, often translucent- **Motion:** may flutter or shift### Fingerprints / oils- **Scale:** smudge-sized, patterned- **Location:** lens front element, filters, glass- **Character:** haze, flare, contrast loss- **Motion:** static### Chemical stains- **Scale:** irregular patches- **Location:** film emulsion, prints- **Character:** color shift, texture change- **Motion:** static, progressive### Mold / biological- **Scale:** organic patterns, veining- **Location:** film, prints, storage- **Character:** color distortion, material degradation- **Motion:** progressive over time### Water / moisture- **Scale:** droplets, streaks, fog- **Location:** lens, film, scanning- **Character:** diffusion, distortion, temporary or permanent- **Motion:** may evaporate or stain## Cross-medium comparison| Contaminant | Optical | Film | Print | Digital ||-------------|---------|------|-------|---------|| Dust | Soft spots | Flecks | Specks | Sensor spots || Scratches | Rare | Common | Possible | Screen damage || Hair | Strands | Strands | N/A | N/A || Oils | Haze | N/A | N/A | N/A || Chemical | Coating damage | Emulsion damage | Paper damage | N/A |## Common stackingContamination stacks with:- age / neglect- poor storage- mechanical wear- environmental exposure## Anti-flattening notesDo not use "dirty" or "grime" vaguely.Specify:- contaminant type- location in image path- optical vs physical damage- temporary vs permanent## SummaryThe contamination family is the material world intruding on the image—matter where it should not be, leaving traces of touch, time, and environment.
