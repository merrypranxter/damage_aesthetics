# DAMAGE PALETTE LOGICThis file documents how damage affects color—shifts, limitations, and emergent palettes from degraded media.## DefinitionDamage palette logic refers to systematic color behaviors in damaged media: shifts, restrictions, contaminations, and emergent color signatures of specific failure modes.## Damage color mechanisms### Chemical shift- Dye fading (selective color loss)- Oxidation (silver mirroring, color cast)- Staining (introduced color from contamination)### Signal limitations- Chroma bandwidth (reduced color fidelity)- Chroma noise (color speckle and instability)- Channel dropout (missing color information)### Compression effects- Chroma subsampling (color resolution reduction)- Quantization (color banding)- Gamut clipping (color range limitation)### Physical contamination- Color cast from stains- Warm/cool shift from mold, chemicals- Toning from environmental exposure## Damage palette categories### The Warm Decay Palette**Sources:** Light leaks, film fade, chemical staining, heat damage**Characteristics:**- Amber, orange, rust dominance- Cyan/blue loss or shift to teal- Cream whites, yellowed highlights- Warm shadow lift**Media:** Aged film, heat-damaged prints, vintage VHS---### The Sick Digital Palette**Sources:** Compression, sensor noise, codec artifacts**Characteristics:**- Magenta/green channel imbalance- Cyan shadows, orange midtones (common error)- Banding in gradients (stepped color)- Chroma speckle in darks**Media:** Low-bitrate video, phone night mode, compressed streams---### The Chemical Stain Palette**Sources:** Processing errors, environmental damage, mold**Characteristics:**- Irregular color patches- Purple/brown mold tones- Green/blue oxidation- Pink/red fixer stains**Media:** Damaged film, poorly stored prints, water damage---### The Phosphor/Screen Palette**Sources:** CRT display, early digital, specific display technologies**Characteristics:**- Cyan/blue bright emphasis- Warm shadow (phosphor afterglow)- Saturated primaries- Scanline color separation**Media:** CRT capture, arcade games, early digital video---### The Toner/Print Palette**Sources:** Xerographic reproduction, print decay**Characteristics:**- Reduced saturation- Cyan/magenta drift- Warm paper base- Toner density variations**Media:** Photocopies, laser prints, fax degradation---## Palette application### AuthenticityUse true-to-medium palettes for documentary effect.### StylizationExaggerate or combine palettes for expressive effect.### HybridizationMix palette signatures for temporal confusion.## Palette severityS1–S2: subtle shift, atmosphericS3: obvious color character, medium-specificS4–S5: extreme shift, near-monochrome or false-color## Prompting palettesSpecify damage color logic:> "warm amber decay from light leaks and film fade, cyan shadows lost to chemical shift, cream highlights from aged paper base"## SummaryDamage palettes are the color of time and failure—each medium's specific chemistry and physics writing their signature in hue and saturation.
