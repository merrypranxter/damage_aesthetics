# HEAT BURN PALETTESThis file documents specific color signatures of heat damage and light/thermal destruction across media.## DefinitionHeat burn palettes refer to the color shifts and signatures associated with thermal damage: fading, browning, charring, and chemical alteration from heat exposure.## Heat damage color progression### Stage 1: Warm shift- Yellowing of whites- Slight orange cast- Reduced blue response### Stage 2: Amber/brown dominance- Deep amber shadows- Brown midtones- Cream to yellow highlights- Blue/cyan severely attenuated### Stage 3: Sepia/monochrome- Near-complete color loss- Brown/black monochrome- Possible color fringing at edges### Stage 4: Char/black- Burned regions to pure black- Possible reddish ember tones- Physical damage visible## Medium-specific heat palettes### Film (acetate/nitrate)**Vinegar syndrome early:** Slight yellowing, vinegar odor**Vinegar syndrome advanced:** Deep amber, emulsion buckling, channel separation**Nitrate decomposition:** Amber to brown, sticky, potentially combustible### Inkjet prints**Heat fade:** Yellow/magenta loss, cyan dominance**Extreme heat:** Paper yellowing, ink spreading, possible blistering### Laser prints**Heat exposure:** Toner shift, paper yellowing, possible fusing artifacts### Magnetic tape**Binder breakdown:** Not primarily color, but associated with age-yellowing of shell## Heat burn stack colorsHeat damage often combines with:- Light fade (accelerated)- Chemical shift (catalyzed)- Physical warping (dimensional change)Resulting palette: warm, reduced range, often nostalgic or tragic affect.## Prompting heat burn> "amber-shifted from heat aging, cream highlights, brown shadow compression, blue channel severely faded, emulsion showing vinegar syndrome tones"## SummaryHeat burn palettes are the color of time accelerated—warm, reduced, melancholy tones of chemistry pushed toward entropy by thermal stress.
