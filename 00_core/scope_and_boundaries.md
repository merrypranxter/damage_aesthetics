# SCOPE AND BOUNDARIESThis file defines what the DAMAGE repo covers, what it partially covers, and what it excludes.## In scopeThe repo includes visual artifact systems arising from:- capture- storage- handling- transfer- compression- transmission- playback- display- optics- print / scan workflows- material decay- chemical alteration- hybrid simulation## Fully in scopeThe following are fully in scope:- VHS and analog tape artifacts- CRT and display artifacts- film damage and development damage- optical contamination- dirty lens effects- vaseline / grease / smearing on glass- chemical stains and burns- sensor noise and bad pixels- digital compression artifacts- file corruption visuals- datamosh-type artifacts- frame ghosting and persistence- recompression decay from web/social pipelines- scan and xerox artifacts- playback instability- generational loss from duplication## Partially in scopeThe following are partially in scope when tied directly to media damage behavior:- atmospheric haze- motion blur- lens flare- bloom- color cast- geometric distortion- abstraction caused by transmission or processingThese are only included when they are tied to medium-specific or cause-specific damage.Example:- "blur" alone is too generic- "grease-smeared lens blur with veiling flare" is in scope## Out of scope by defaultThese are generally outside the repo unless framed through real artifact logic:- generic surreal distortion- generic glitch art with no medium grounding- clean cyberpunk UI overlays- abstract digital patterning- non-media texture design- fantasy corruption with no system analogy- ordinary aesthetic soft-focus photography unless tied to contamination or failure## Important boundary ruleThe repo is about **damage signatures**, not merely **visual weirdness**.A thing should not be included just because it looks odd or broken.It should have at least one of the following:- a medium relationship- a process history- a causal mechanism- a recognizable artifact behavior- a consistent simulation target## Authenticity vs utilityThe repo includes both:- physically authentic artifact descriptions- artistically useful approximationsHowever, useful stylization should remain anchored to believable behavior whenever possible.This means:- invention is allowed- vagueness is not preferred- exaggeration is allowed- flattening all artifact types into one blob is not## Boundary examples### In scope- tape tracking wobble- lens fingerprint haze- scanline moiré from screen capture- light leak on film- JPEG ringing around edges- chroma bleed from analog video- frame echo caused by interframe prediction collapse### Out of scope- "cool distortion"- "glitchy neon look"- "retro grain"- "corrupted aesthetic"- "dreamy blur" with no medium logic## Operational boundary testAsk these questions:1. What medium does this belong to?2. What process caused it?3. What visible behavior defines it?4. What neighboring artifacts does it resemble but differ from?5. Could it be classified clearly enough to use in prompting or simulation?If the answer is mostly "no," the concept is too vague and should be refined before inclusion.
