# NEIGHBORING AESTHETICSThis file maps how DAMAGE relates to adjacent aesthetic territories without collapsing into them.## DAMAGE vs glitch artGlitch art is broader and often stylized, graphic, or computationally generated.DAMAGE is specifically about failure signatures of media and capture systems, whether real or simulated.Glitch art says: "cool broken computer."DAMAGE says: "this image has been injured by a real or believable system."Overlap exists, but DAMAGE insists on medium and cause specificity.## DAMAGE vs vaporwave / retro media nostalgiaVaporwave often uses clean nostalgia props: CRTs, scanlines, magenta/cyan, VHS labels.DAMAGE is less about retro mood and more about forensic degradation.Not just "old media."More like "old media that got dragged behind a fucking truck."## DAMAGE vs lo-fiLo-fi can be soft, charming, imperfect, cozy.DAMAGE is harsher, stranger, more pathological, more forensic.Lo-fi wants to be loved.DAMAGE wants to be examined.## DAMAGE vs found footage horrorOverlap, sure. But horror uses damage to imply narrative threat.DAMAGE itself is medium-agnostic. It can be beautiful, psychedelic, forensic, mournful, ecstatic, or neutral.The affect is not predetermined.## DAMAGE vs analog fetish aestheticsSome people just want grain, leaks, and softness because it's "warm."DAMAGE wants the full damage zoo:optical, chemical, magnetic, signal, codec, sensor, print, xerox, compression, dirt, heat, duplication, decay.Warmth is optional. Truth is required.## Key distinctions summary| Neighbor | DAMAGE difference ||----------|-------------------|| Glitch art | medium-specific failure, not just "broken digital" || Vaporwave | forensic degradation, not clean nostalgia props || Lo-fi | pathological/strange, not cozy/imperfect || Horror | medium-agnostic, not threat-coded || Analog fetish | full damage taxonomy, not just "warm grain" |## Why this mattersWithout these boundaries, DAMAGE becomes:- generic "glitchy" sludge- retro filter pack- horror aesthetic dependency- lo-fi comfort zoneThe boundaries keep it sharp, specific, and structurally useful.
