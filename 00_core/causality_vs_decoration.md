# CAUSALITY VS DECORATIONThis file addresses the tension between damage as meaningful system behavior and damage as surface ornament.## The problemDamage can be used two ways:1. **Causal**: the artifact implies a real or believable process2. **Decorative**: the artifact is applied for visual effect without system logicBoth are valid. But they should be distinguished.## Causal damageCharacteristics:- implies a medium- implies a process- implies a history- behaves according to system constraints- stacks logically with related artifacts- has legibility consequences that make senseExample:"VHS chroma bleed caused by analog signal bandwidth limitations, appearing as color drag on horizontal motion, commonly co-occurring with luma softness and tracking instability."## Decorative damageCharacteristics:- applied for mood or texture- may mix artifacts without system logic- may exaggerate beyond physical plausibility- may prioritize visual effect over believabilityExample:"RGB split, scanlines, and film grain layered together because it looks cool, regardless of whether this combination could occur in any real media chain."## The repo positionDAMAGE prefers causal logic as the foundation.Decorative use is allowed, but should ideally:- acknowledge its hybrid nature- remain anchored to recognizable artifact families- avoid claiming false authenticity## When decoration becomes problematic- when it flattens all damage into generic "glitchy" sludge- when it misattributes causes (film burn on a digital-only image)- when it ignores medium incompatibility (VHS tracking errors on a scanned photograph)- when it pretends to be authentic while being arbitrary## The hybrid zoneMuch creative work lives here: stylized damage that references real systems but pushes them beyond realism.This is fine. Mark it as:- "stylized VHS-inspired damage"- "hybrid analog-digital artifact stack"- "exaggerated film damage simulation"Don't pretend it's documentary when it's poetry.## Practical guidanceWhen documenting an artifact, ask:- what system produces this?- could this occur in real media?- what would normally accompany it?- is this being used causally or decoratively?When using an artifact in prompts or shaders, ask:- am I building a believable system?- am I mixing incompatible systems without acknowledgment?- does the severity match the claimed cause?## SummaryCausality is the spine.Decoration is the skin.The repo needs both, but the spine keeps the body from collapsing.
