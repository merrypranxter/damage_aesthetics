# DAMAGE DEFINITION## Working definition**DAMAGE** is a cross-media system for classifying and describing visible degradation, contamination, corruption, and failure signatures in images, video, display systems, film, scans, recordings, and related visual media.It includes both:1. **real artifact behaviors** produced by specific media conditions, and2. **believable simulations** of those behaviors used for artistic or analytical purposes.## Core ideaA damaged image is not simply "low quality."It has undergone one or more processes that have altered the visibility, integrity, stability, texture, color, timing, or legibility of the original signal or captured scene.DAMAGE studies those processes as visible evidence.## What counts as damageFor the purposes of this repo, "damage" includes:- physical contamination- optical contamination- material wear- heat or age-related degradation- chemical alteration- signal instability- playback errors- display defects- compression artifacts- decoding failures- transmission artifacts- duplication loss- storage decay- temporal echoing- frame persistence- color corruption- geometry corruption- partial data loss- stylized simulation of the above## Cross-media scopeDAMAGE spans all of the following:- analog video- digital video- still digital images- film- photography optics- CRT and display systems- camera sensors- scans / xerox / print workflows- network and platform recompression- replay and capture chainsIt is intentionally broader than any single medium.## Visual emphasisDAMAGE focuses on **visible artifact behaviors**.This means the repo is concerned with things like:- what the artifact looks like- what pattern it makes- how it moves or persists- where in the frame it appears- how strongly it affects readability- what kind of material or signal history it implies## Two main branches### 1. True damageArtifacts caused directly by real conditions in the medium or processing chain.Examples:- scratched negative- VHS tracking instability- dirty lens- hot pixels- scanner streaks- JPEG macroblocks- datamosh prediction residue### 2. Simulated damageArtifacts produced intentionally to imitate believable damage.Examples:- shader-generated scanline distortion- fake VHS chroma bleed- composited dust and scratches- simulated tape skew- intentionally introduced codec breakupSimulation is allowed here, but only when it is informed by recognizable damage families.## Minimum requirements for something to belong in DAMAGEAn effect belongs in DAMAGE if at least one of the following is true:- it is a recognizable failure signature of a medium- it is a contamination or wear pattern that affects perception- it is a structural artifact of encoding, playback, capture, or display- it is a stylized but grounded simulation of a known media injury- it interacts meaningfully with legibility, timing, color, or surface perception## ExclusionsDAMAGE does not automatically include:- arbitrary abstract distortion with no medium relationship- decorative RGB split with no artifact logic- generic "retro vibes"- generic blur, unless tied to a specific cause- random texture overlays without process coherence- broad horror aesthetics unless the underlying damage behavior is relevant## Core promise of the repoThis repo promises to treat damage as:- structured- classifiable- translatable- stackable- medium-aware- visually specificIt does not flatten all brokenness into one bucket.
