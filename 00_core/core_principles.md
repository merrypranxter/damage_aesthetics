# CORE PRINCIPLESThese are the guiding principles of the DAMAGE repo.## 1. Medium specificityDamage should be tied to the medium that produces it.A film burn is not a codec error.A tape smear is not optical blur.A hot pixel is not grain.Medium matters.## 2. Cause awarenessVisible damage is treated as the consequence of a process.The repo prioritizes asking:- what caused this?- how does that cause appear visually?- what related artifacts come with it?Cause prevents empty decoration.## 3. Behavior over vibeArtifacts should be described as behaviors, not vague moods.Good:- horizontal tearing- chroma crawl- edge ringing- halation halo- frame persistenceWeak:- glitchy- broken- retro- damaged-lookingBehavior is more precise and more useful.## 4. Stack logicMost real damage happens in combination.The repo should preserve the relationships between co-occurring artifacts rather than treating all damage types as isolated effects.## 5. Legibility mattersDamage changes the readability of an image.Some artifacts:- obscure subjects- erase edge clarity- create false motion- compete with focal points- shift emotional toneThese consequences matter for composition and use.## 6. Simulation should stay groundedStylized or simulated damage is welcome, but should remain anchored to real or believable artifact behavior unless deliberately marked as abstract.## 7. Taxonomy must resist driftThe repo must actively resist collapsing into vague categories such as:- glitch- retro- lo-fi- corrupted- creepy VHSThose phrases are only useful when unpacked into specific behaviors.## 8. Cross-media comparison is usefulDifferent media can express related damage families in different ways.Example:- film grain- digital sensor noise- analog video snowThese are related but not identical.The repo should allow comparison without flattening difference.## 9. Damage is structural, not just decorativeA good damage description should reveal something about:- medium- history- process- system failure- translation chainDamage is not just "stuff on top."It is often part of how the image exists.## 10. Brokenness can be beautiful without becoming emptyThe repo allows beauty, stylization, and expressive exaggeration.But it should never lose the structural clarity of what kind of damage is being described.
