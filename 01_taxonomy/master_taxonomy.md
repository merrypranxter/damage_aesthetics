# MASTER TAXONOMYThis file defines the primary classification structure of DAMAGE.The taxonomy is designed around five major axes:1. medium2. artifact family3. cause / process4. severity5. stack behavior---## I. Medium domains### A. Analog videoIncludes:- VHS- composite video artifacts- tape instability- broadcast-related signal issues- analog playback errors### B. CRT and display systemsIncludes:- scanline structure- phosphor behavior- convergence problems- geometry distortion- screen-capture interference### C. Digital videoIncludes:- codec failure- streaming damage- frame prediction artifacts- packet loss- datamosh-type behavior### D. Still digital imageIncludes:- JPEG artifacts- color quantization- banding- file corruption- oversharpening / resampling scars### E. FilmIncludes:- grain- scratches- dust- burns- light leaks- emulsion and development damage### F. Photography / opticsIncludes:- dirty lens- vaseline / grease smear- haze- flare- diffusion- focus failure- aberration### G. Camera sensorIncludes:- sensor noise- hot/stuck/dead pixels- rolling shutter- readout errors- gain-related ugliness### H. Print / scan / xeroxIncludes:- toner drag- misregistration- scan bands- repeated copy decay- printhead failures### I. Playback / display chainIncludes:- pausing artifacts- frame skipping- freeze/repeat behaviors- buffering signatures- replay instability### J. Web / social / transcode systemsIncludes:- screenshot-of-screenshot decay- platform recompression- upload-transcode damage- screen recording artifacts---## II. Damage families### 1. NoiseRandom or semi-random distributed signal interference.Subtypes:- grain- static- snow- luma noise- chroma noise- sensor speckle### 2. Smear / drag / bleedDirectional spreading, dragging, or leakage of image information.Subtypes:- tape smear- chroma bleed- motion drag- compression smear- optical smear### 3. Tearing / sync instabilityFrame or field instability caused by timing or signal weakness.Subtypes:- horizontal tear- vertical roll- skew- jitter- sync loss### 4. Compression breakageArtifacts caused by insufficient or unstable encoding / decoding.Subtypes:- macroblocking- mosquito noise- ringing- band collapse- prediction residue### 5. Surface contaminationMatter or residue interfering with capture or display.Subtypes:- dust- scratches- fingerprints- haze residue- stains- mold / corrosion traces### 6. Light / exposure failureDamage caused by light overload, insufficient exposure, or material interaction with light.Subtypes:- clipping- bloom- halation- light leaks- overexposure veil- underexposure murk### 7. Color failureColor channel misbehavior or corruption.Subtypes:- misalignment- cast shift- false color- channel dropout- chroma instability- palette collapse### 8. Geometry failureSpatial deformation of image structure.Subtypes:- warping- curvature- skew- stretch- malformed block geometry### 9. Temporal / memory artifactsPersistence or repetition of visual information across time.Subtypes:- ghost frames- afterimages- motion echoes- persistence trails- freeze / repeat loops---## III. Cause domainsDamage may originate from:- physical handling- dirty surfaces- bad optics- heat- age- storage conditions- chemical exposure- playback instability- signal weakness- transfer chain mismatch- transcoding- recompression- data corruption- display-capture mismatch- deliberate simulation---## IV. Severity scaleAll damage types should be mappable to rough severity levels:### S0 — noneNo meaningful visible artifact.### S1 — traceBarely perceptible; mostly atmospheric.### S2 — mildPresent but not dominant.### S3 — moderateClearly visible and stylistically active.### S4 — severeStrong impact on readability and composition.### S5 — catastrophicThe image is partially collapsing, unreadable, or deeply transformed.---## V. Stack behaviorArtifacts often appear in combination.### A. Native stacksStacks commonly produced by the same medium or failure type.Example: VHS- chroma bleed- tracking error- head-switching noise- luma instability### B. Causal stacksStacks produced by one underlying cause.Example: dirty lens- haze- veiling flare- softness- contrast loss### C. Hybrid stacksStacks combining multiple media or causes.Example:- CRT capture moiré- platform recompression- screen-recorded buffer stutter- lens glare### D. Stylized stacksDeliberately exaggerated combinations used for creative effect.These should still preferably reference real artifact families even when pushed harder than natural.---## Taxonomy ruleEvery major artifact concept in the repo should ideally be classifiable across at least these fields:- medium- family- cause- severity range- common stack partnersThis is the basic skeleton of DAMAGE.
