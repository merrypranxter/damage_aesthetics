# PHOSPHOR BLOOM AND TRAILSThis file covers brightness spread, luminous softening, and persistence effects associated with CRT phosphor behavior.Phosphor behavior is one of the most distinctive and most frequently butchered aspects of CRT aesthetics. It is not just "glow." It involves energy response, persistence, and the way bright information excites and lingers on the screen.## DefinitionPhosphor bloom and trails refer to the visible expansion, softening, and persistence of bright image information caused by phosphor excitation and display response in CRT systems.This includes:- bright-region blooming- luminous edge softening- persistence trails- afterglow- temporal smear on fast bright motion- display-based glow behavior## Core visual identityPhosphor behavior tends to feel:- luminous- energetic- alive- screen-native- temporally sticky- bright-edge weighted- more display-based than lens-basedIt differs from blur because it is driven by brightness response and persistence, not just loss of focus.## Common visible traits### 1. Bright-edge bloomBright elements spread slightly beyond their strict edge boundaries.### 2. Luminous softeningHigh-contrast bright regions feel inflated, glowing, or electrically excited.### 3. Phosphor persistencePrevious bright positions may linger briefly, especially in motion.### 4. Temporal trailsMoving bright objects may leave faint display-native afterimages or streaks.### 5. Highlight swellingThin bright lines or UI elements may appear thicker or more radiant than their actual source.### 6. Brightness-dependent spreadThe stronger the brightness, the stronger the bloom.## Main causes / mechanisms- phosphor excitation and decay behavior- beam energy and brightness response- screen persistence characteristics- display refresh structure- camera/display mismatch during screen recapture- overdriven bright elements on CRT screens## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- light / exposure failure- temporal / memory artifactsSecondary:- raster structure- color behavior- display recapture interference## Common stack partnersPhosphor bloom and trails often combine with:- CRT raster visibility- convergence error- slight barrel distortion- analog signal softness- moiré when filmed- bright UI graphics- subtitle glow- motion smear from recapture## Bloom vs trails### BloomA mainly spatial effect.Brightness spreads around bright sources.### TrailsA mainly temporal effect.Previous bright positions linger after change.They often coexist, but should not be treated as the same thing.## Phosphor bloom vs other glow phenomena### Phosphor bloom vs lens flarePhosphor bloom is display-native.Lens flare comes from optical system.### Phosphor bloom vs halationHalation is film-material behavior.Phosphor bloom is screen/display behavior.### Phosphor bloom vs digital bloomDigital bloom is often a post-process approximation or clipping glow.Phosphor bloom has display-specific screen characteristics.### Trails vs motion blurTrails are persistence-based and often brighter.Motion blur is broader and not necessarily brightness-weighted.## Severity range### S1–S2- slight glow on bright edges- faint persistence on high-contrast motion### S3- obvious luminous swelling- visible bright-object trails- bright text or graphics visibly blooming### S4–S5- strong glow contamination- aggressive persistence smearing- major loss of edge accuracy around bright regions- bright moving objects leaving dominant ghost paths## Legibility effectsAt low levels:- makes screens feel alive- enhances brightness richness- adds warmth and authenticityAt high levels:- thickens type and thin lines- obscures edge precision- causes false motion readings- merges adjacent bright elements- softens UI or graphic detail## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesPhosphor bloom and trails may feel:- electric- hypnotic- dreamy- arcade-like- sacred- unstable- glowing- memory-soaked- alive## Confusion risks### Trails vs ghost framesGhost frames are broader temporal residue across whole image.Phosphor trails are display-persistence specific.### Bloom vs hazeHaze veils the whole image.Phosphor bloom is brightness-weighted and localized.### Bloom vs compression smearCompression smear redistributes encoded detail.Phosphor bloom is display glow behavior.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- CRT phosphor bloom around bright edges- cathode-ray persistence trails- luminous bright-object afterglow- blooming arcade-screen highlights- display-native bright-edge spread- phosphor persistence on moving light formsAvoid:- just add glow- neon blur- old TV glowThose are too vague and usually wrong.## Shader / FX translation notesGood simulation strategies may include:- brightness-thresholded blur/spread- temporal accumulation for bright regions- selective persistence on high-luma motion- screen-space glow with restrained radius- subtle color weighting based on channel intensity- optional raster-aware applicationThe key is that the effect should feel:- brightness-driven- display-native- not like camera blur or fog## SummaryPhosphor bloom and trails are what happen when light on a CRT refuses to stay perfectly bounded in space or time.It glows.It lingers.It lives.
