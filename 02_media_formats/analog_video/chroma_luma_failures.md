# CHROMA LUMA FAILURESThis file documents the separation, weakness, and instability of color (chroma) and brightness (luma) signals in analog video systems.Analog video often carries chroma and luma on separate pathways or with different bandwidth allocations, leading to characteristic failures.## DefinitionChroma/luma failures refer to visible artifacts caused by:- insufficient bandwidth for color information- signal separation errors- crosstalk between color and brightness- differential degradation of chroma vs luma pathways## Core visual identityThese failures feel:- color-unstable- edge-dirty- horizontally smeared- electrically compromised- like color is "leaking" or "dragging"## Common visible traits### Chroma bleedColor spreads beyond object edges, especially horizontally.Red and blue areas most affected.### Chroma noiseColor speckle or instability in saturated regions.### Luma softnessFine detail lost, edges mushy, textures compressed.### Chroma/luma delayColor and brightness slightly misaligned, producing colored edge fringes.### Color crawlMoving chroma instability, especially in NTSC systems (often called "NTSC artifacting").## Causes- limited chroma bandwidth in composite video- differential signal degradation- poor cable quality- generation loss in duplication- chroma noise from weak signal## Severity rangeS1–S2: slight color softness, minor edge bleedS3: obvious chroma drag, clear luma softnessS4–S5: severe color breakup, near-monochrome luma with color smear## Confusion risks### Chroma bleed vs chromatic aberrationBleed is signal-based; aberration is optical lens behavior.### Luma softness vs defocusSoftness is signal/detail loss; defocus is optical.## SummaryChroma and luma failures are the signature of analog video's electrical compromises—color sacrificed for bandwidth, detail softened for signal stability.
