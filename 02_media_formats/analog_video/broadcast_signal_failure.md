# BROADCAST SIGNAL FAILUREThis file covers analog and quasi-analog broadcast-related signal instability, interference, and transmission failure behaviors that extend beyond VHS tape damage.This includes:- weak signal artifacts- interference- unstable synchronization- reception failure- transmission contamination- signal breakup during live or recorded analog pathways## DefinitionBroadcast signal failure refers to visible image disruption caused by instability in signal transmission, reception, synchronization, or conversion in television and analog-video distribution systems.This is distinct from:- pure tape wear- pure optical contamination- purely digital compression artifactsIt lives in the realm of:- transmission- signal strength- interference- timing- broadcast chain instability## Core visual identityBroadcast failure tends to feel:- electrically unstable- externally interrupted- horizontally structured- intermittent- atmospheric in a technical way- more "signal sick" than "materially worn"Where VHS often feels like damaged memory, broadcast failure feels like damaged reception.## Common visible traits### 1. Static burstsRandom or semi-random noisy interruptions overtaking part or all of the frame.### 2. Horizontal tearing and rollThe frame loses stable lock and parts of the image slip or shear horizontally.### 3. Sync lossThe image may jump, slide, roll, or partially collapse because timing relationships fail.### 4. Signal snowWeak reception produces dancing noise fields, low-contrast interference, and degraded picture quality.### 5. Ghosting / multipath echoDuplicate or offset image information appears due to reflected signal paths or transmission timing.### 6. Flicker and instabilityBrightness or frame coherence may pulse or fluctuate.### 7. Interference bandsBroad or narrow horizontal bands may cross the image, often with different contrast or noise characteristics.### 8. Partial dropoutParts of the image may vanish into unstable noise or fail to resolve.## Main causes- weak reception- interference from nearby electronic sources- bad tuning- antenna problems- cable degradation- broadcast chain instability- poor conversion between systems- sync or timing mismatch- signal reflections / multipath## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- tearing / sync instability- noise- temporal artifacts- geometry failureSecondary:- color failure- smear / bleed## Broadcast failure vs VHS failureThese overlap, but are not identical.### VHS damageFeels:- tape-based- worn- stored- duplicated- playback-mechanical### Broadcast failureFeels:- live or transmission-based- externally interrupted- electrically unstable- reception-related- sync-sensitiveA stack can contain both, but they should not be collapsed into one undifferentiated mess.## Common stack partnersBroadcast failure often combines with:- CRT display bloom- screen moiré- analog color instability- tape capture of off-air signal- platform recompression after later digitization- subtitle chatter or edge instability if rebroadcast/recorded repeatedly## Severity range### S1–S2- faint snow- slight flicker- minor reception instability### S3- visible signal noise- mild ghosting- rolling interference bands- partial lateral instability### S4–S5- strong static takeover- sync collapse- broad horizontal tearing- large image dropout zones- severe multipath or unstable rolling frame## Legibility effectsBroadcast signal failure tends to attack:- continuity- temporal stability- edge anchoring- frame lock- contrast reliabilityAt severe levels, it can make the image feel like it is constantly trying to reassemble itself.## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesBroadcast failure may feel:- eerie- unreachable- liminal- unstable- technical- distant- surveillance-like- accidental- haunted by interruption## Confusion risks### Broadcast static vs digital noiseBroadcast noise usually feels field-like and unstable, not compressed or blocky.### Sync loss vs glitch displacementBroadcast sync failure is timing-based and scan-structured, not algorithmic chunk corruption.### Multipath ghosting vs digital frame echoMultipath feels transmission-reflected; digital frame echo feels computationally buffered.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- weak analog broadcast reception- unstable TV signal with rolling interference bands- partial sync loss and signal snow- analog transmission ghosting- broadcast instability with flickering frame lock- weak off-air reception textureAvoid:- TV glitch- static TV look- old television effectThose phrases are lazy and too vague.## Shader / FX translation notesBroadcast failure simulation may involve:- intermittent noise field injection- horizontal band displacement- unstable sync offset- global flicker- ghost image overlay with slight offset- partial dropout zones- soft signal-decay transitionsThis effect is more about unstable resolution and lock than about block structure.## SummaryBroadcast signal failure is the visible behavior of a picture being damaged in transit.It does not feel stored.It feels interrupted.
