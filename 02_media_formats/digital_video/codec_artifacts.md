# CODEC ARTIFACTSThis file documents visual damage behaviors produced by digital video compression, encoding, decoding, bitrate constraints, and prediction systems.Codec artifacts are one of the dominant contemporary damage languages because so much visual culture now passes through compression pipelines. They are not random corruption. They are the visible consequence of computational shortcuts, bitrate sacrifice, motion prediction, and imperfect reconstruction.## DefinitionCodec artifacts are visible distortions, simplifications, instabilities, and reconstruction errors caused by video compression and decompression processes.This includes:- macroblocking- mosquito noise- banding- edge ringing- detail simplification- interframe prediction residue- temporal smearing- motion breakup- low-bitrate texture collapse## Core visual identityCodec damage tends to feel:- computational- segmented- simplified- predictive- temporally weird- edge-sensitive- bandwidth-constrained- contemporaryUnlike analog video damage, codec artifacts often feel like the image is being approximated rather than physically degraded.## Common visible traits### 1. MacroblockingThe image breaks into visible block regions, especially in motion, shadows, gradients, or low-bitrate areas.### 2. Mosquito noiseFine chattering junk appears around edges, text, or high-contrast boundaries.### 3. Edge ringingEcho-like halos or overshoot contours appear around detailed edges.### 4. Gradient band collapseSmooth transitions break into visible tonal steps or flat bands.### 5. Texture simplificationFine detail gets smeared, flattened, or replaced by approximate texture.### 6. Temporal prediction residueMotion leaves partial remnants, melting, mispredicted structures, or lagging ghosts.### 7. Motion breakupFast movement becomes blocky, unstable, or partially dissolved.### 8. Detail starvationHigh-frequency textures such as hair, foliage, glitter, smoke, or grain become muddy or lost.## Main causes / mechanisms- low bitrate- aggressive compression settings- repeated transcodes- poor streaming conditions- limited bandwidth- interframe prediction errors- insufficient keyframes- quantization loss- resaving already compressed media- platform upload degradation## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- compression breakage- temporal / memory artifacts- geometry failure, in block form- color failureSecondary:- noise-like edge chatter- motion smear / drag## Codec artifacts vs other digital ugliness### Codec artifacts vs file corruptionCodec artifacts can happen in normal playback under compression stress.File corruption implies deeper data damage or invalid structure.### Codec artifacts vs datamoshingDatamoshing is a more extreme prediction-abuse behavior.General codec artifacts include ordinary low-bitrate damage as well.### Codec artifacts vs sharpening halosSharpening halos can coexist, but codec ringing is part of compression reconstruction error.### Codec artifacts vs analog signal failureCodec damage is computational and block/prediction structured, not signal-based.## Common stack partnersCodec artifacts often combine with:- platform recompression- screen recording softness- screenshot-of-screenshot decay- digital noise in dark areas- text edge chatter- scaling and resampling damage- motion estimation weirdness- temporal freeze residue## Severity range### S1–S2- mild edge chatter- subtle banding- minor macroblocking in gradients or motion### S3- obvious block segmentation- clear temporal smear- visible edge junk- detail collapse in fast movement### S4–S5- large-scale block breakup- severe motion reconstruction failure- broad texture destruction- image regions turning into unstable compressed sludge## Legibility effectsAt low levels:- slightly cheapens the image- flattens richness- reduces fine detail fidelityAt high levels:- destroys text- abstracts moving bodies- breaks facial detail- creates false geometry- makes gradients ugly as sin- causes temporal confusion## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesCodec artifacts may feel:- cheap- contemporary- degraded- unstable- streamed- disposable- accelerated- platform-poisoned- computationally sickThey often imply:- circulation- upload- bandwidth limits- digital overhandling## Confusion risks### Macroblocks vs mosaic filtersMacroblocks are compression scars, not stylized censorship blocks.### Temporal smear vs motion blurCodec smear is predictive failure, not natural motion integration.### Ringing vs analog ghostingRinging is edge reconstruction chatter, not signal echo.### Banding vs posterizationThese overlap, but banding in codec contexts often arises from compressed gradients and reduced smoothness over time.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- low-bitrate video compression artifacts- visible macroblocking
