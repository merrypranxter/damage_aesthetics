# DATAMOSH FAMILYThis file covers datamoshing and related interframe-prediction abuse aesthetics within compressed digital video.Datamoshing sits inside the broader world of codec artifacts, but it deserves its own file because it is one of the most distinctive and creatively useful temporal corruption behaviors in digital video.## DefinitionDatamoshing refers to visible image corruption behaviors produced by disrupting, exploiting, or artistically abusing interframe compression and motion-prediction systems in digital video.This can include:- frame residue- motion-vector drift- smeared object persistence- melting transitions- shape borrowing between frames- temporal block migration- predictive ghost fields- motion contamination across scene cutsDatamosh-like behavior can be produced intentionally or encountered accidentally in damaged digital files.## Core visual identityDatamoshing tends to feel:- gooey- predictive- haunted by previous frames- temporally contaminated- block-structured but fluid- computationally delirious- transition-poisoned- motion-possessedUnlike ordinary compression damage, datamoshing makes the image behave like it cannot stop using old motion information in the wrong places.## Common visible traits### 1. Frame residueShapes from previous frames remain present even after scene content changes.### 2. Motion-vector smearingMovement stretches image regions according to stale or misapplied prediction data.### 3. Transition meltCuts or transitions fail cleanly, causing one scene to slither into the next.### 4. Temporal contaminationMotion history infects new objects, backgrounds, or camera movements.### 5. Block migrationCompressed block regions move, deform, or persist in ways tied to motion prediction.### 6. Shape borrowingNew objects inherit texture or motion logic from old objects.### 7. Ghost fieldsThe image seems populated by predictive leftovers rather than straightforward motion blur.## Main causes / mechanisms- disrupted interframe prediction- missing or altered keyframes- manipulated compressed streams- forced decode mismatch- transcode weirdness- motion-vector misuse- deliberate datamoshing workflows- scene-cut prediction failure## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- temporal / memory artifacts- compression breakage- geometry failure- smear / drag / bleedSecondary:- color failure- block-region instability## Datamosh vs normal codec damage### Normal codec damageUsually appears as:- macroblocks- banding- edge chatter- detail loss### DatamoshPushes into:- temporal hallucination- motion residue as structure- transition collapse- moving ghosts and block-slimeDatamoshing is not merely "more compression."It is a more specific type of temporal corruption.## Datamosh vs motion blurMotion blur integrates motion into a camera-like streak.Datamoshing misapplies prior frame information and prediction vectors.Motion blur says: "the subject moved."Datamosh says: "the codec remembered wrong and now reality is wearing old motion like a skin."## Common stack partnersDatamosh often combines well with:- macroblocking- low-bitrate softness- edge ringing- packet loss- repeated transcode damage- screenshot or screen-record workflows- CRT capture if deliberately hybridized- color channel breakup## Severity range### S1–S2- faint temporal residue- slight predictive smearing during cuts or movement### S3- obvious datamosh trails- merges scenes- turns bodies into migrating prediction fields- makes movement feel hallucinatory or wrong- partially destroys scene structure## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesDatamoshing may feel:-
