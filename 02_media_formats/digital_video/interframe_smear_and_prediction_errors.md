# INTERFRAME SMEAR AND PREDICTION ERRORSThis file documents temporal artifacts caused by motion prediction and interframe compression—when the codec tries to save bits by reusing and warping previous frame information.## DefinitionInterframe smear and prediction errors occur when video compression systems (P-frames, B-frames) misapply motion vectors, fail to update regions correctly, or create temporal residue through prediction failure.## Core visual identityThese artifacts feel:- temporally sticky- motion-infected- prediction-wrong- smeared through time- like the past won't let goUnlike motion blur (integration of real motion), this is computational misremembering.## Common visible traits### 1. Motion trail residueMoving objects leave partial copies or smears behind them.### 2. Prediction breakupFast motion causes regions to fragment or misalign.### 3. Temporal ghostingPrevious frame content persists inappropriately in new frame regions.### 4. Vector misalignmentMotion appears to slide or shear in block-shaped chunks.### 5. Update failureRegions that should change remain static or partially static.### 6. Motion-compensated weirdnessBackgrounds warp or shift to follow incorrect motion predictions.## Causes- insufficient motion vector precision- scene changes without keyframes- fast motion exceeding prediction range- occlusion errors (objects covering/uncovering regions)- bitrate starvation preventing proper updates- deliberate prediction abuse (datamoshing)## Relationship to datamoshingDatamoshing is the extreme, artistic exploitation of prediction errors.This file covers the broader family of prediction-related artifacts, including accidental and natural compression behavior.## Severity rangeS1–S2: faint trails, subtle motion weirdnessS3: obvious smearing, prediction artifacts visibleS4–S5: severe temporal corruption, motion hallucination, scene breakdown## Confusion risks### Interframe smear vs motion blurMotion blur is optical/time-based integration.Interframe smear is computational prediction residue.### Prediction error vs ghost framesGhost frames are full-frame persistence.Prediction errors are regional, block-based, or vector-based.## Prompting notesUseful:- interframe prediction smear- motion vector residue- P-frame corruption trails- temporal update failure- motion-compensated block drift## SummaryInterframe artifacts are the visible cost of compression trying to predict the future by warping the past.When it guesses wrong, time itself seems to smear.
