# MACROBLOCKING AND BAND FAILUREThis file documents two of the most visible and recognizable digital compression artifacts: block segmentation and gradient banding.These are often the first signs of "digital damage" that casual viewers recognize, but they have specific structural causes that distinguish them from other visual degradation.## Definition**Macroblocking** refers to the visible division of the image into block-shaped regions (typically 8x8 or 16x16 pixels) due to coarse quantization and discrete cosine transform (DCT) compression.**Banding** refers to the visible step-like transitions in smooth gradients due to insufficient bit depth or aggressive quantization in compression.## Core visual identity### MacroblockingFeels:- tiled- segmented- computational- region-based- edge-defined### BandingFeels:- stepped- posterized- smoothness collapsed- tonal staircaseBoth feel like the image has been forced through a coarse grid.## Common visible traits### Macroblocking- visible square or rectangular regions- especially obvious in motion, shadows, skies- edges between blocks may show discontinuities- texture within blocks simplified or flattened- block boundaries may flicker or shift temporally### Banding- smooth gradients break into visible steps- skies become striped or layered- shadows lose subtle transition- color regions show discrete jumps- may animate or shift with compression keyframes## Causes### Macroblocking- low bitrate forcing coarse quantization- insufficient bits allocated to complex regions- motion compensation errors- DCT coefficient truncation- aggressive compression profiles### Banding- insufficient color depth (8-bit or less)- quantization of smooth tonal regions- repeated compression passes- lifting crushed blacks or compressing highlights in post- display limitations amplifying compressed steps## Severity range### MacroblockingS1–S2: subtle block edges in motion or shadowS3: obvious block structure in multiple regionsS4–S5: dominant block grid, severe texture loss, block animation overwhelming content### BandingS1–S2: faint steps in smooth gradientsS3: clear stair-stepping in skies, shadows, or color fieldsS4–S5: severe posterization, gradient collapse into distinct bands, color regions flattened## Artifact family relationshipsPrimary:- compression breakage- geometry failure (macroblocking)- color failure (banding)Secondary:- temporal instability (shifting blocks)- noise (quantization artifacts)## Confusion risks### Macroblocking vs pixelationPixelation is uniform upscaling of low resolution.Macroblocking is compression-specific block artifacts at any resolution.### Banding vs natural gradientNatural film or optical gradients have grain or noise that prevents perfect smoothness.Banding is artificially stepped.### Banding vs posterization from bit depthRelated, but compression banding often has temporal instability and block alignment.## Stack partnersMacroblocking often with:- mosquito noise at block edges- temporal smear- edge ringingBanding often with:- crushed blacks or clipped whites- color cast shifts- noise in darker steps## Prompting notesUseful:- banding artifactsToo vague without compression context.## SummaryMacroblocking and banding are the visible grid of compression trying to save bits by sacrificing smoothness and detail.The image becomes tiled and stepped—mathematically efficient, visually wounded.
