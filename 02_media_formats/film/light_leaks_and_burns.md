# LIGHT LEAKS AND BURNSThis file covers luminous intrusion, overexposure damage, and chemically or physically destructive bright-field injury associated with film media and adjacent analog systems.These effects are often romanticized into vague "film vibes," but they have distinct visual logic and different levels of severity. A gentle light leak is not the same thing as catastrophic burn-through. This file keeps those separate while preserving their shared family relationship.## DefinitionLight leaks and burns refer to unwanted or destructive bright-field intrusions caused by:- accidental light exposure- compromised camera or film loading conditions- damaged seals- projection or processing errors- intense localized heat/light injury- deliberate burning or overexposure effects in film materialThis family includes:- soft leak veils- edge intrusions- flare-like exposure contamination- bright color blooms caused by unintended exposure- destructive burn marks- frame-edge blowout- melt-like light injury## Core visual identityThis family tends to feel:- invasive- luminous- material- destructive or romantic depending on severity- edge-biased- overexposed- leaking rather than simply glowingUnlike controlled cinematic lighting, these effects feel like the medium has been breached.## Main categories### 1. Light leaksUnwanted light entering the camera or film path, often producing:- red/orange/yellow veils- edge glows- partial frame washout- irregular luminous intrusions- soft blooming contaminationOften associated with:- faulty seals- loading exposure- camera body leakage- accidental handling exposure### 2. Burn-through / destructive burnsSevere localized damage producing:- white-hot blown regions- darkened char-like borders- frame holes or near-holes- melting or eaten-away appearance- catastrophic loss of image informationOften associated with:- projection accidents- direct heat- intentional burning- advanced material damage### 3. Overexposure breach fieldsBright contamination that may not physically burn the film but visually overwhelms it.These may appear as:- giant washed highlight zones- blooming veils- clipped frame areas- exposure flooding from one side or region## Common visible traits### 1. Edge-biased intrusionMany leaks originate from frame edges, corners, or seams.### 2. Warm color behaviorLight leaks often skew warm:- orange- red- amber- yellow- pinkish burn tones### 3. Irregular shape languageThese are usually not clean circles or neat gradients.They often feel:- torn- feathered- asymmetrical- ragged- drifting### 4. Image displacement by brightnessLeaks and burns overwhelm existing image structure rather than merely sitting on top.### 5. Material vulnerabilityAt stronger levels, the effect feels like the medium is being injured, not just overexposed.## Main causes / mechanisms- compromised light seals- improper loading/unloading- accidental exposure during handling- projection gate overheating- direct flame or heat contact- damaged camera body or film container- processing mishaps- intentional distressing## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- light / exposure failureSecondary:- surface/material damage- color failure- geometry/material collapse in severe burns## Common stack partnersThis family often combines with:- grain- dust and scratches- halation- faded color- gate weave- chemical stain- distressed archival texture- edge wear- scan softness- flicker## Severity range### S1–S2- faint warm edge leaks- partial corner washout- restrained luminous intrusion### S3- obvious frame-edge leaks- major warm contamination- significant local detail loss- leak becomes a compositional actor### S4- severe blowout- large bright breach regions- strong loss of image content- destructive-looking edges### S5- catastrophic burn-through- image eaten away by brightness/heat- frame partially destroyed- the damage becomes the main event## Legibility effectsAt low levels:- add vulnerability- suggest accidental exposure- create romance, instability, or warmthAt high levels:- erase subjects- distort focal hierarchy- destroy edge clarity- overwhelm composition- create massive dead zones of unreadability## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesThese effects may feel:- nostalgic- ecstatic- fragile- intimate- feverish- damaged- romantic- catastrophic- sacred- terminalLight leaks can feel like accidents.Burns can feel like violence.Both involve the medium failing to contain light properly.## Confusion risks### Light leak vs lens flareA leak is a breach or accidental exposure.Flare is optical behavior.### Burn vs bloomBloom spreads brightness while preserving medium integrity.Burn implies stronger breach or material damage.### Leak vs halationHalation hugs bright image edges.Leak intrudes from outside or from compromised exposure.### Overexposure vs burn-throughOverexposure may flood but preserve.Burn-through implies more severe material or near-material damage.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- warm film light leak bleeding in from the frame edge- accidental analog exposure breach- orange-red leak wash across the negative- projector-burned frame edges- destructive film burn-through with charred borders- blown-out exposure intrusion eating into the image- ragged overexposed film breachAvoid:- film burn effect- vintage leak- retro orange glowToo generic unless specified.## Shader / FX translation notesSimulation may involve:- irregular edge-origin masks- warm color-biased exposure flooding- soft-to-hard transition control- local clipping and contrast erasure- feathered intrusion geometry- optional burned-border darkening for severe cases- animated drift for moving-image versionsImportant:Light leaks should not look like clean Instagram filters.They should feel accidental, asymmetrical, and physically plausible unless deliberately stylized.## SummaryLight leaks and burns are what happen when film loses its ability to keep light in its proper fucking place.
