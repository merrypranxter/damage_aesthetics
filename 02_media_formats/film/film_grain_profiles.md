# FILM GRAIN PROFILESThis file documents film grain as a material image texture and damage-adjacent behavior within photochemical imaging.Film grain is one of the most abused and flattened concepts in image aesthetics. People use "grain" to mean everything from tasteful texture to random dirt to digital noise to fake Instagram bullshit. In this repo, film grain is treated specifically as a material, stock-dependent, photochemical image behavior.Film grain is not always "damage" in the sense of error or failure. But within DAMAGE it matters because:- it affects legibility- it affects texture and tonal behavior- it is often intensified, abused, degraded, or mis-simulated- it is frequently confused with unrelated noise systems## DefinitionFilm grain refers to the visible particulate image texture resulting from the photochemical structure and response of film stock.It is influenced by:- stock type- film speed- exposure- development- enlargement- scan quality- duplication- age and degradationGrain is not merely a surface overlay. It is part of the image's material body.## Core visual identityFilm grain tends to feel:- particulate- organic- stochastic- embedded in the image- tonally alive- stock-dependent- more material than electronicUnlike many digital noise systems, grain often feels woven into the tonal body of the image rather than floating on top of it.## Common visible traits### 1. Particulate textureThe image contains fine to coarse irregular texture that is neither perfectly uniform nor purely random.### 2. Tonal livelinessFlat regions often feel subtly alive rather than mathematically flat.### 3. Shadow/body richnessGrain often becomes especially perceptible in midtones, shadows, or enlarged regions.### 4. Stock variabilityDifferent stocks produce different grain characters:- fine and tight- soft and creamy- rough and chunky- hard and gritty### 5. Enlargement sensitivityGrain becomes more obvious when film is enlarged, scanned badly, or pushed.### 6. Motion shimmer in projection or transferIn moving-image contexts, grain may produce a delicate living surface quality.## Main causes / mechanisms- silver halide crystal behavior or related stock chemistry- film speed and emulsion design- underexposure / push processing- development conditions- stock age- duplication loss- scan resolution and transfer process- enlargement and print chain## Grain profile categories### 1. Fine grain- tight- subtle- elegant- low-intrusion- high legibility preservationOften associated with:- slower stocks- good exposure- clean scans- higher image fidelity### 2. Moderate grain- clearly visible- texturally rich- materially expressive- still controlledOften ideal for:- medium realism- atmospheric texture- grounded film identity### 3. Coarse grain- chunky- gritty- aggressive- visibly particulate- increasingly dominantOften associated with:- faster stocks- low light- push processing- rougher scans or transfers### 4. Distressed / abused grain- intensified by age, duplication, poor scan, overprocessing, or material wear- may merge with dirt, scratches, instability, or tonal collapse- sits near the boundary between native film texture and damage accumulation## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- noiseSecondary:- light/exposure behavior- surface contamination, if aged or dirty- temporal texture, in projected film or moving-image transfer## Film grain vs neighboring concepts### Film grain vs sensor noiseGrain is photochemical.Sensor noise is electronic/readout based.### Film grain vs analog video snowSnow is unstable signal interference.Grain is image-native photochemical texture.### Film grain vs compression chatterCompression chatter clusters around coding pressure points.Grain is distributed image texture with stock character.### Film grain vs dustDust sits on/over the image path.Grain is within the image body.## Common stack partnersGrain often combines with:- scratches- dust- gate weave- halation- light leaks- soft focus- duplication loss- scan softness- chemical stain or age shift- print wear## Severity range### S0–S1- very fine, barely perceptible grain- mostly tonal liveliness### S2- clearly visible but restrained grain- adds material identity without overpowering content### S3- obvious coarse grain- major texture contributor- starts to affect edge and tonal readability### S4–S5- extremely rough, enlarged, distressed grain- may overwhelm detail- often merged with scan, duplication, or age-related degradationImportant: grain can be aesthetically central without being "catastrophic." Severity here refers to how strongly it alters readability and surface behavior.## Legibility effectsAt low levels:- enriches tone- adds tactile realism- prevents sterile smoothnessAt high levels:- softens fine detail- roughens skin, fabric, smoke, gradients- can obscure subtle features- may dominate quiet regions of the frame## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesFilm grain may feel:- human- tactile- memory-soaked- warm- gritty- intimate- cinematic- lived-in- aged- physically realDepending on profile, it can also feel:- harsh- dirty- exhausted- documentary- sacred- pulverized## Confusion risks### "Grain" used to mean any textureWrong. This repo uses it specifically.### Fake grain overlaysOften too uniform, too lazy, too disconnected from the tonal structure.### Grain confused with blurGrain adds particulate activity; blur reduces detail by spreading/softening.### Grain confused with dustDust is contamination. Grain is image body.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- photochemical film grain- fine-grain stock texture- coarse enlarged grain structure- gritty pushed-film grain- materially embedded grain field- dense analog grain in shadow regions- stock-like particulate image bodyAvoid:- add grain- film texture- vintage grainToo sloppy unless specified.## Shader / FX translation notesFilm-grain simulation should ideally:- avoid perfect uniformity- relate to tonal structure- vary subtly across luminance regions- feel embedded rather than overlaid- avoid reading like sensor speckle or TV snowGood simulation may involve:- luminance-aware stochastic texture- slight temporal modulation in video contexts- scale control tied to "stock size / enlargement" metaphors- restrained channel coupling unless stylized## SummaryFilm grain is the particulate breathing of photochemical image making.It is not noise.It is not dirt.It is the body of the medium showing itself.
