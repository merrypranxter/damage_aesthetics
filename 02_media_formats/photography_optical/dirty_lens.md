# DIRTY LENSThis file covers contamination of the lens or optical front element by dirt, oils, fingerprints, residue, moisture, or grime, and the resulting perceptual damage to the image.Dirty-lens damage is one of the most important optical categories in DAMAGE because it is both common in real-world capture and endlessly useful in stylized simulation. It creates a very different result from signal failure, film abrasion, or codec breakage. It is optical contamination, not media encoding damage.## DefinitionDirty lens refers to visible image degradation caused by contaminants on the lens surface or near-front optical path.This may include:- dust- fingerprints- skin oils- grease- smudges- dried residue- moisture droplets- grime- environmental film on glassThe resulting image effects are optical, meaning they arise from light being scattered, softened, redirected, or partially blocked before it reaches the sensor or film.## Core visual identityDirty-lens effects tend to feel:- soft- veiled- intimate- tactile- low-contrast- contaminated- physically close to the viewer- more like "seeing through something"This family often affects highlights strongly and may make the whole image feel touched, breathed-on, or handled.## Common visible traits### 1. Contrast lossThe whole image or parts of it lose crisp contrast, especially around bright regions.### 2. Veiling glare / hazeA translucent contamination layer seems to float across the view, often making blacks less black.### 3. Highlight spreadBright points may bloom, smear, or flare more than usual.### 4. Smudge patternsLocalized soft patches, streaks, arcs, or thumbprint-like intrusions may become visible.### 5. Uneven softnessThe image may not blur uniformly; some areas are more contaminated than others.### 6. "Touched glass" feelingThe image feels bodily contaminated rather than mechanically failed.## Main contamination types### 1. Fingerprints / skin oilsCommonly produce:- patchy haze- crescent or thumbprint forms- bright-region flare spread- greasy glare contamination### 2. Dust / grit on lensMay produce:- small soft spots- slight flare anomalies- subtle general contaminationUsually less dramatic than sensor dust unless severe.### 3. Grease / residue filmCan create:- broad veiling softness- luminous smears- major highlight diffusion- low-contrast glow### 4. Moisture / condensationMay produce:- fogginess- soft diffuse veils- glow around lights- uneven clearing patterns### 5. Dirt / grime buildupMay combine:- haze- reduced clarity- ugly flare behavior- dulling of image edges## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- surface contamination- light / exposure failure- smear / drag / bleed, in some residue casesSecondary:- local blur / focus degradation- flare contamination## Dirty lens vs neighboring concepts### Dirty lens vs soft focusSoft focus is optical defocus or aesthetic design.Dirty lens is contamination on the optical path.### Dirty lens vs lens flareFlare may result from clean optics under certain lighting.Dirty lens often worsens flare and makes it uglier, broader, and more unpredictable.### Dirty lens vs Vaseline smearVaseline is a more specific, often deliberate grease/diffusion technique.Dirty lens is the broader contamination category.### Dirty lens vs sensor dustSensor dust tends to show as sharper/fixed image-plane spots depending on aperture and focus.Dirty lens contamination often feels broader, glowier, and more highlight-reactive.## Common stack partnersDirty lens often combines with:- bloom- flare- overexposure- condensation- backlit scenes- soft focus- film grain- VHS softness, in hybrid chains- phone-camera night glare- screen recapture glare## Severity range### S1–S2- slight haze- mild contrast loss- faint highlight contamination### S3- obvious smudges- strong veiling glare- broad greasy softness- visible touched-glass feel### S4–S5- major image contamination- severe milkiness- highlights spreading wildly- broad unreadable zones from smear or residue## Legibility effectsAt low levels:- softens harshness- adds tactility- enhances intimacy or realism- makes bright scenes feel humid, used, or bodilyAt high levels:- kills contrast- obscures detail- muddies text and small features- swallows edge clarity- makes the image feel visually suffocated## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesDirty-lens effects may feel:- intimate- gross- vulnerable- dreamy- sweaty- humid- bodily- low-rent- documentary- filthy- tender- too closeDepending on use, it can read as either poetic or disgusting as hell.## Confusion risks### Dirty lens vs haze in the sceneScene haze exists in the world.Dirty-lens haze exists on the viewing apparatus.### Dirty lens vs diffusion filterA diffusion filter is controlled and usually uniform.Dirty lens is contaminated, irregular, and often uglier.### Dirty lens vs compression softnessCompression damage is mathematical.Dirty lens is optical and physical.### Dirty lens vs bloomBloom may be one consequence of dirty optics, but bloom alone is not enough to define dirty-lens damage.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- dirty lens haze across bright regions- fingerprint-smudged optics causing veiling glare- greasy front-element contamination- touched-glass highlight bloom- low-contrast optical grime- smudged lens diffusion with bodily residue- humid dirty-lens softness in backlightAvoid:- blurry lens- cinematic haze- dreamy filterToo vague, too clean, too full of shit.## Shader / FX translation notesSimulation may involve:- localized diffusion masks- bright-region spread- contrast flattening- irregular smudge geometry- broad translucent veils- asymmetric glare contamination- angle/light-sensitive behavior in more advanced systemsGood dirty-lens simulation should feel:- irregular- tactile- bodily- not like a uniform Gaussian blur slapped over the whole frame## SummaryDirty-lens damage is what happens when the apparatus of seeing gets touched by the world—or by someone's greasy little fingers.
