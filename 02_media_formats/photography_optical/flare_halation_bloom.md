# FLARE, HALATION, BLOOMThis file covers three related but distinct bright-energy behaviors that are constantly confused in aesthetic discourse.They all involve brightness escaping intended boundaries.They are not the same damn thing.This file exists to keep those differences clear while mapping their relationships.## DefinitionFlare, halation, and bloom are visual phenomena in which bright light expands, contaminates, or modifies surrounding image information beyond a strict edge boundary.They differ by mechanism:- **flare** = optical reflections/scattering in the lens system- **halation** = film-material light spread and glowing edge behavior- **bloom** = brightness swelling, clipping spread, or luminous softening in sensor/display/post contexts## Why these get confusedAll three can look like:- glow- softened bright edges- highlight contamination- luminous spread- reduced local clarityBut:- where they originate- how they spread- what they imply- what medium they belong toare all different.## 1. FLARE### DefinitionFlare is unwanted or aesthetically useful light contamination caused by reflection, scattering, or internal bounce within the optical system.### Core feel- optical- angular or geometric- source-dependent- lens-bound- often directional### Common traits- streaks- veiling glare- ghost orbs- washed contrast around bright sources- directional light intrusion- source-linked artifacts### Typical causes- strong light entering lens- dirty optics- multiple lens surfaces- low sun / direct bright source- poor coatings or deliberate vintage glass behavior### Key identityFlare feels like the lens got invaded by a light source.## 2. HALATION### DefinitionHalation is a film-specific or film-adjacent bright-edge glow caused by light scattering within or behind the emulsion structure, often producing colored fringes or warm red/orange edge glow around strong highlights.### Core feel- film-native- edge-hugging- material- chemically/structurally embedded- often warm### Common traits- bright edges developing soft halos- glow around backlit highlights- reddish or warm edge bleed in some stocks/workflows- luminous fringe around high-contrast bright forms### Typical causes- light passing through emulsion and scattering/backing interaction- stock-specific material response- strong highlights on film### Key identityHalation clings to bright image structures like the stock itself is radiating around them.## 3. BLOOM### DefinitionBloom is broader brightness spread or swelling beyond intended boundaries, often associated with sensor overload, display response, post-processing, or clipped/high-luma image regions.### Core feel- luminous- swelling- brightness-dependent- not necessarily optical- not necessarily film-specific### Common traits- glowing highlights- thickened bright lines- localized wash around intense brightness- soft expansion of light regions- edge loss around clipped forms### Typical causes- digital post-process- sensor clipping- CRT phosphor response- aggressive highlight treatment- bright-display response### Key identityBloom makes light get fat and spread.## Shared traitsAll three may:- reduce edge clarity- create glow around bright sources- flatten local contrast- contribute to dreaminess, heat, or intensity- make highlights feel larger than they areThat shared territory is why people constantly mash them together.## Distinguishing questionsAsk:1. Is the effect clearly tied to the lens and source angle?   - likely flare2. Is it hugging bright edges in a film-native way?   - likely halation3. Is it broader highlight swelling from brightness intensity or display/sensor/post behavior?   - likely bloom## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- light / exposure failureSecondary:- surface contamination, when dirty optics worsen flare- temporal behavior, in phosphor/display bloom cases- color failure, when halation or flare shifts hue## Common stack partners### Flare stacks well with:- dirty lens- Vaseline smear- haze- backlighting- low sun- moisture on optics### Halation stacks well with:- film grain- light leaks- overexposure- backlit edges- archival film texture### Bloom stacks well with:- CRT phosphor behavior- sensor clipping- overexposed video- diffusion- soft display aesthetics## Severity range### S1–S2- restrained bright edge spread- light source contamination that remains controlled### S3- obvious glow fields- major bright-edge swelling- clear contrast loss around light sources### S4–S5- aggressive glare contamination- severe highlight takeover- large zones of washed-out brightness- image structure consumed by luminous spill## Legibility effectsAt low levels:- enriches highlights- adds atmosphere- suggests medium identityAt high levels:- destroys edge precision- merges nearby bright features- obscures typography- causes blown focal regions- erases local form boundaries## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesThese effects may feel:- sacred- dreamy- feverish- radiant- erotic- holy- cheap- overcooked- ecstatic- cinematic- spiritually friedThe emotional result depends heavily on severity and medium context.## Confusion risks### Halation mislabeled as bloomVery common, especially in film simulation contexts.### Flare mislabeled as halationAlso common, especially when warm edge glow is present.### Bloom used as catchall for all glowBullshit. Too broad to be useful on its own.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- lens flare contamination from off-axis bright source- film halation hugging bright edges- digital highlight bloom swelling around overexposed forms- warm halation fringe on backlit film- dirty-optics flare veil- CRT-style bloom on bright UI elementsAvoid:- glowy- cinematic glow- dreamy light leak bloom flare thingThat's just word salad.## Shader / FX translation notes### Flare simulation- source-dependent lens ghosts/streaks- angle-responsive veiling- optional contamination weighting from dirty optics### Halation simulation- edge-biased glow- bright-thresholded fringe spread- warm/red edge emphasis when appropriate- restrained radius### Bloom simulation- thresholded bright-area expansion- luminance-driven blur/spread- optional clipping-based edge loss- display or sensor context awareness## SummaryFlare, halation, and bloom all let light escape its boundaries.But they escape for different reasons, in different mediums, and with different visual characters.
