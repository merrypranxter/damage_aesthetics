# VASELINE DIFFUSION SMEARThis file covers deliberate grease-based optical diffusion, especially the classic act of smearing Vaseline or similar substances on glass, filters, lens protectors, or other optical surfaces to create soft, glowing, contaminated image effects.This sits near dirty-lens territory, but it deserves its own file because it is often:- intentional- more stylized- more spatially shaped- more dramatically glow-oriented- more obviously "smeared" than ordinary grimeThis is not accidental dirt. This is optical sabotage with aesthetic intent.## DefinitionVaseline diffusion smear refers to image degradation and stylization produced by a grease-like translucent substance applied to part or all of an optical path.Typical effects include:- dreamy softness- glowing highlights- smeared specular spread- edge softening- localized blur fields- directional diffusion- bodily / cosmetic / contaminated glam hazeThe substance does not simply blur the image.It scatters and diffuses light in irregular, tactile, highly highlight-reactive ways.## Core visual identityVaseline smear tends to feel:- creamy- greasy- glam- bodily- dreamy- smeared by hand- intimate- low-contrast- perversely luxurious- contaminated on purposeUnlike ordinary dirty-lens contamination, it often has a more performative and shaped visual quality.## Common visible traits### 1. Highlight bloom and swellingBright areas spread outward with milky or creamy softness.### 2. Hand-shaped smear fieldsThe diffusion may have:- arcs- finger-wipe patterns- crescent swirls- smeared streaks- patchy transparent zones### 3. Uneven image claritySome regions stay clearer while others dissolve into glow or softness.### 4. Contrast milkingBlacks lift, edge contrast weakens, and the image feels bathed in a greasy veil.### 5. Beauty-through-contaminationThe image may look both softened and physically handled.### 6. Directional dragDepending on how the substance was applied, bright details may drag, feather, or streak in a particular direction.## Main causes / mechanisms- grease or petroleum jelly applied to
