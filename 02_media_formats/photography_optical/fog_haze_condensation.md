# FOG, HAZE, CONDENSATIONThis file covers optical obscuration caused by vapor, moisture, microdroplets, atmospheric veil, and condensation-like diffusion occurring either in the scene or on the viewing apparatus.This category matters because "haze" gets used for too much random bullshit. Here, the repo distinguishes moisture and veil behavior as a set of specific optical obscuration systems with different causes and characters.## DefinitionFog, haze, and condensation refer to visible image softening and contrast loss caused by suspended or surface-adjacent light-scattering particles.This may include:- atmospheric haze in the scene- lens fogging- condensation on glass- microdroplet scatter- warm/cold transition misting on optics- vapor veil around highlightsThese effects alter how light reaches the sensor or film by scattering and reducing directional coherence.## Core visual identityThis family tends to feel:- veiled- humid- soft- breathy- diffuse- low-contrast- milky- vaporous- sometimes cold, sometimes sweatyUnlike grease smear, this family often feels more airborne or environmental.## Main categories### 1. Atmospheric hazeLight-scattering matter exists in the scene itself.Traits:- distance softening- lowered contrast with depth- washed far backgrounds- space filled with luminous veil### 2. Lens fog / fogged opticsMoisture condenses on the viewing apparatus.Traits:- broad milky softness- shifting clearing zones- highlight halos- image looks breathed-on or thermally shocked### 3. Condensation droplets / microdroplet veilFine moisture beads or thin water film alter the image.Traits:- soft bloom- local diffusion- irregular translucent shapes- wet-glass or humid-camera feel## Common visible traits### 1. Contrast suppressionBlacks lift, whites spread, and edges soften.### 2. Brightness-dependent diffusionLights and highlights grow larger, softer, and more luminous.### 3. Depth lossSpatial separation weakens as the image becomes more uniformly veiled.### 4. Uneven clearingCondensation especially may produce partial clear zones or streaky recovery patterns.### 5. Vapor presenceThe image can feel like it is being seen through breath, weather, humidity, or thermal shock.## Main causes / mechanisms- humid air- atmospheric particulates- warm-to-cold lens transitions- cold-to-warm lens transitions- breath on glass- weather- steam- moisture on protective surfaces- environmental diffusion layers## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- light / exposure failure- surface contamination, when on opticsSecondary:- softness / diffusion- glare / flare enhancement## Fog/haze/condensation vs neighboring concepts### Haze vs dirty lensHaze may be in the scene or on optics.Dirty lens implies residue/contamination on the viewing apparatus.### Condensation vs Vaseline smearCondensation is vapor/moisture-based, usually less greasy and less shaped.### Haze vs bloomHaze veils space.Bloom is localized brightness spread.### Atmospheric haze vs lens fogAtmospheric haze lives in the world.Lens fog lives on the apparatus.## Common stack partnersThis family often combines with:- flare- bloom- backlighting- dirty lens- low-contrast color grading- overexposure- soft focus- headlights or point lights- moisture streaks- night photography/video## Severity range### S1–S2- faint veil- slight distance wash- mild humid glow### S3- obvious atmospheric or optical softness- reduced contrast across much of frame- lights visibly enlarged by moisture### S4–S5- major milkiness- severe scene obscuration- broad unreadable soft fields- image nearly swallowed by wet veil or heavy atmospheric fog## Legibility effectsAt low levels:- softens and romanticizes- adds atmosphere- implies weather, humidity, distance, or bodily presenceAt high levels:- erases fine edges- kills microcontrast- hides depth cues- enlarges lights into blobs- buries subject separation## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesThis family may feel:- eerie- intimate- wintery- feverish- humid- dreamy- melancholy- nocturnal- fragile- suffocating- holy- sweaty as hellDifferent subtypes swing differently:- atmospheric haze can feel sublime- lens fog can feel bodily and immediate- condensation can feel wet, nervous, and alive## Confusion risks### Atmospheric fog vs optical fogOne is in the world. One is on the apparatus.### Condensation vs dirty lens grimeCondensation is moisture-reactive and often temporary or shifting.Grime is residue and usually more stable.### Haze vs generic blurHaze is light scatter with contrast and depth consequences, not just defocus.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- humid lens fog with broad milky highlight spread- atmospheric haze reducing distant contrast- condensation-softened optics with partial clearing zones- breathed-on glass diffusion- vaporous low-contrast veil- wet-lens microdroplet glow- steam-hazed image planeAvoid:- soft dreamy fog- moody haze- cinematic blurThat's lazy bullshit.## Shader / FX translation notesSimulation may involve:- depth-aware contrast wash for atmospheric haze- broad low-frequency diffusion layers- highlight-weighted halo growth- partial-opacity clearing masks- microdroplet or moisture pattern modulation- shadow lift and local contrast suppressionImportant:Condensation should not just be generic Gaussian blur.It should feel wet, uneven, and environmentally responsive.## SummaryFog, haze, and condensation are what happen when the space between seeing and the seen fills up with light-scattering vapor.
