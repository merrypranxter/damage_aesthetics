# SENSOR NOISEThis file documents electronic noise behaviors associated with digital camera sensors and related capture systems.Sensor noise is one of the most common modern image degradations and one of the easiest to describe lazily and badly. It is not just "grain but digital." It has its own electronic logic, tonal distribution, and failure character.## DefinitionSensor noise refers to visible random or semi-random image variation produced by electronic capture systems during image acquisition, especially under conditions of:- low light- high ISO / gain- thermal stress- small sensors- poor readout conditions- weak signal levelsThis can affect:- luminance- color channels- shadow stability- edge cleanliness- temporal consistency in video## Core visual identitySensor noise tends to feel:- electronic- pixel-tethered- shadow-heavy- often color-speckled- unstable in dim regions- less material than film grain- more capture-system sick than image-body richIt often reads as the sensor struggling to pull signal out of darkness.## Common visible traits### 1. Shadow speckleDark regions break into dancing or fixed noise particles.### 2. Chroma noiseColor flecks appear where clean color should exist, especially in shadows and low-light regions.### 3. Luma roughnessBrightness values fluctuate, making quiet regions feel granular or unstable.### 4. Weak-detail instabilityFine details in dark or underexposed areas feel noisy and uncertain.### 5. Temporal crawling in videoNoise may shimmer or crawl from frame to frame rather than sitting like stable texture.### 6. Amplified ugliness under gainAs ISO or signal amplification rises, the image may become:- dirtier- thinner- rougher- less color-stable## Main causes / mechanisms- low-light capture- ISO amplification / gain increase- thermal noise- sensor design limitations- small photosites- readout noise- underexposure followed by lifting in post- poor denoising pipelines- cheap sensors or stressed video compression after noisy capture## Typical artifact families involvedPrimary:- noise- color failureSecondary:- compression breakage, if the noisy footage is later compressed badly- temporal instability in video## Sensor noise profile categories### 1. Fine luma noise- subtle brightness roughness- often less ugly than chroma noise- may feel acceptable or documentary-like### 2. Chroma speckle- ugly color blotches or dots- especially common in shadows- often the most immediately digital-looking subtype### 3. High-gain sludge- noisy, weak, murky image body- poor color integrity- unstable dark detail- often paired with compression pain### 4. Temporal crawling noise- noise pattern shifts frame-to-frame- produces restless shadow movement- especially noticeable in compressed digital video## Sensor noise vs neighboring concepts### Sensor noise vs film grainSensor noise is electronic and often shadow/color weighted.Film grain is photochemical and materially distributed.### Sensor noise vs compression noiseSensor noise comes from capture.Compression noise comes later from encoding decisions.### Sensor noise vs analog staticAnalog static is signal contamination.Sensor noise is acquisition system struggle.### Sensor noise vs dustDust sits as contamination.Noise lives in the signal itself.## Common stack partnersSensor noise often combines with:- underexposure- clipped highlights- bad denoising smears- low-bitrate codec artifacts- phone night-mode weirdness- rolling shutter issues- sharpened edge halos- dirty lens glare in bad lighting- lifted blacks- color instability## Severity range### S1–S2- mild shadow roughness- restrained low-light texture- minor chroma speckle### S3- obvious noise in dark and midtone regions- clear color speckle- unstable low-light image body### S4–S5- aggressive noisy sludge- severe shadow breakup- major chroma ugliness- image integrity compromised by gain noise and weak signal## Legibility effectsAt low levels:- adds documentary realism- suggests low-light struggle- prevents overly clean digital sterilityAt high levels:- destroys shadow detail- makes skin look diseased- introduces color uncertainty- competes with subject matter- signals cheap or stressed capture## Emotional / perceptual qualitiesSensor noise may feel:- documentary- stressed- cheap- immediate- low-light desperate- electronic- modern- compromised- night-sick- gain-pushedIt rarely feels nostalgic or warm.It often feels like the machine is working too hard.## Confusion risks### Sensor noise vs film grainDifferent causes, different distributions, different moods.### Chroma noise vs color castNoise is speckled variation.Cast is uniform shift.### Temporal noise vs motionNoise crawls randomly.Motion follows structure.## Prompting notesUseful phrases:- high-ISO sensor noise in shadows- chroma speckle from gain amplification- electronic noise crawling in low light- digital capture noise under exposure stress- noisy shadow detail from small sensorAvoid:- digital grain- noise texture- camera noiseToo vague. Specify sensor, shadow, chroma, or gain context.## Shader / FX translation notesSimulation may involve:- luminance-thresholded noise (heavier in shadows)- chroma channel speckle- temporal shimmer/crawl- ISO/gain responsive intensity- sensor-size metaphor (smaller = noisier)Should not feel like film grain or analog static.Should feel electronic, pixel-based, and shadow-weighted.## SummarySensor noise is the electronic sweat of a digital camera trying to see in the dark.It is not grain.It is not static.It is the sensor's struggle made visible.
