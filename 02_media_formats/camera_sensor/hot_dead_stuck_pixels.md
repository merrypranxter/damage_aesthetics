# HOT, DEAD, STUCK PIXELSThis file documents specific pixel-level sensor failures: pixels that misbehave due to manufacturing defects, age, radiation, or thermal stress.Unlike general sensor noise, these are discrete, localized, and often persistent failures of individual photosites.## Definition- **Hot pixels**: pixels that read as brighter than they should, especially under long exposure or high temperature- **Dead pixels**: pixels that read as black or near-black, unresponsive to light- **Stuck pixels**: pixels that always read a fixed color value regardless of actual light## Core visual identityThese feel:- pixel-specific- point-like- persistent or semi-persistent- hardware-defective- electronic- sometimes dancing (hot pixels under thermal stress)- sometimes fixed (dead/stuck pixels)They are the "scars" of the sensor grid itself.## Visible traits### Hot pixels- bright single-pixel or small-cluster points- worse in long exposures- worse at high temperatures- may come and go- often red, white, or blue### Dead pixels- black or dark single pixels- permanent hardware failure- may be mapped out by camera firmware- visible as dark specks### Stuck pixels- fixed color (often red, green, or blue)- always on regardless of scene- may blink in video- very distracting if in center of frame## Causes- manufacturing defects- sensor age and wear- cosmic radiation (especially in astrophotography)- thermal stress- long exposure heat buildup- physical sensor damage## Severity rangeS1: few scattered pixels, mostly in shadows or edgesS2: noticeable point defects, some in important regionsS3: multiple prominent bad pixels, distractingS4–S5: severe pixel damage, clusters, or widespread hot pixel fields## Confusion risks### Bad pixels vs dust on sensorDust is larger, softer, changes with aperture.Bad pixels are sharp, point-like, fixed.### Hot pixels vs stars in astrophotographyHot pixels don't move with camera motion.Stars do.### Stuck pixels vs compression artifactsStuck pixels are persistent across frames.Compression artifacts shift and change.## Stack partnersOften with:- long exposure noise- high-ISO noise- thermal noise patterns- sensor banding## Prompting notesUseful:- hot pixel noise from long exposure- dead pixel artifacts in sensor- stuck red pixel in center frame- thermal pixel defects in night photography## SummaryThese are the individual casualties of the sensor grid—pixels that have given up, gotten stuck, or gone rogue.
