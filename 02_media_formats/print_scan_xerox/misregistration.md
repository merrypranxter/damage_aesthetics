# MISREGISTRATIONThis file documents color separation errors in multi-pass printing and scanning systems—when cyan, magenta, yellow, and black (or RGB) channels fail to align perfectly.## DefinitionMisregistration refers to visible color channel offset in printed or scanned images, producing colored fringes, ghost edges, or shifted image components.## Core visual identityMisregistration feels:- mechanically precise in its error- color-fringed- offset in specific directions- print-process specific- sometimes rhythmic or patterned## Common visible traits### Color fringing- cyan/magenta/yellow edges around black shapes- rainbow borders on high-contrast edges- directional color shift (often horizontal or vertical)### Ghost displacement- faint offset copies of image elements- "shadow" images in wrong color channels- text appearing in multiple colors slightly separated### Patterned errors- banding at specific intervals- moiré from screen angles interacting- beat patterns in repeated textures## Causes- mechanical tolerance in multi-pass systems- paper stretch or shrinkage between passes- drum or belt positioning errors- thermal expansion in fusing- scanning alignment issues- cheap or aging equipment## Severity rangeS1–S2: subtle fringe on high-contrast edgesS3: obvious color separation, visible ghostingS4–S5: severe displacement, image readability compromised, abstract color separation## Confusion risks### Misregistration vs chromatic aberrationMisregistration is mechanical/print; aberration is optical/lens-based.### Misregistration vs chroma bleedBleed is signal leakage; misregistration is physical alignment error.## Stack partnersOften with:- toner drag- dot gain or loss- screen moiré- copy-of-copy decay## Prompting notesUseful:- CMYK misregistration fringing- color separation ghosting- print registration error- offset color channel displacement## SummaryMisregistration is the visible seam where color channels failed to marry—the mechanical divorce of cyan, magenta, yellow, and black.
