# COPY OF COPY DECAYThis file documents the generational degradation that occurs when printed or xeroxed images are repeatedly copied, scanned, and reprinted.## DefinitionCopy-of-copy decay refers to the accumulated loss, noise, distortion, and artifact buildup that occurs through successive generations of analog or analog-digital-analog reproduction.## Core visual identityThis decay feels:- progressively worse- generationally fatigued- compounded by multiple systems- historically layered- sometimes accidentally artistic## Common visible traits### Generation 1–2- subtle softening- slight contrast loss- minor noise increase### Generation 3–5- obvious blur accumulation- contrast flattening- noise amplification- edge degradation- detail simplification### Generation 6+- near-abstraction- severe information loss- pattern dominance over content- "xerox of xerox of xerox" aesthetic- accidental texture fields## Accumulated artifactsEach generation may add:- toner drag patterns- misregistration- scanner banding- moiré from screen angles- paper texture- edge darkening or lightening- contrast compression## Causes- optical degradation in scan/print cycle- tonal range compression- noise addition at each stage- alignment errors compounding- paper and toner variations- resolution loss## Severity rangeMeasured by generation and accumulated damage rather than single-image severity.G1–G2: S1–S2G3–G5: S3–S4G6+: S4–S5, often approaching abstraction## Confusion risks### Copy decay vs single-generation damageDecay is cumulative and layered; single-generation is immediate.### Digital copy vs analog copy decayDigital copying (file duplication) is lossless.Analog or scanned-copying is lossy and accumulates.## Stack partnersInherently stacked with:- all print/scan artifacts- toner issues- misregistration- scanner banding- paper texture## Prompting notesUseful:- 5th-generation xerox decay- copy-of-copy degradation- generational print loss- accumulated scan/print artifacts## SummaryCopy-of-copy decay is the archaeology of reproduction—each generation burying the original under layers of mechanical interpretation and loss.
