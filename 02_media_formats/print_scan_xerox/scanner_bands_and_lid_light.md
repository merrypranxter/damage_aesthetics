# SCANNER BANDS AND LID LIGHTThis file documents artifacts specific to flatbed and feed scanner capture—particularly banding from sensor movement and light leakage from improper lid sealing.## DefinitionScanner bands are regular or irregular stripes caused by sensor readout variation, calibration issues, or mechanical movement inconsistencies.Lid light is unwanted light entering the scan platen from edges or gaps, causing edge brightening or uneven exposure.## Core visual identity### Scanner bands- regular horizontal or vertical stripes- tied to sensor movement direction- may be faint or severe- sometimes color-specific (RGB channel bands)### Lid light- edge-brightened regions- usually top or side edges- soft gradient into center- "leaked" appearance## Common visible traits### Bands- periodic stripes across image- may be faint tonal variation or strong lines- often more visible in uniform areas (sky, paper)- can be color-banded (cyan, magenta, yellow shifts)### Lid light- brighter edges fading to darker center- asymmetrical (usually one edge or corner)- soft, diffuse, unlike deliberate vignette- may show color cast from ambient light## Causes### Bands- sensor calibration drift- LED or CCD variation across array- movement speed inconsistency- dust on calibration strip- driver or software issues### Lid light- lid not fully closed- warped original or platen gap- scanning 3D objects with lid open- ambient light color affecting edges## Severity rangeS1–S2: faint banding, slight edge lightS3: obvious stripes or edge glowS4–S5: severe banding dominating image, major light leak washing out edges## Confusion risks### Scanner bands vs print moiréBands are capture artifacts; moiré is print-screen interaction.### Lid light vs lens vignetteLid light is uneven and leaky; vignette is optical and symmetrical.## Stack partnersOften with:- paper texture emphasis- dust on scanner glass- moiré from printed originals- color cast from light source## Prompting notesUseful:- flatbed scanner banding artifacts- scanner lid light leak- CCD readout stripes- uneven platen illumination## SummaryScanner artifacts are the mechanical breath of the scanning process—bands from the sensor's journey across the image, light from the world's intrusion into the sealed dark box.
