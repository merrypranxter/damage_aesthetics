# DAMAGE MANIFESTODAMAGE is not the absence of quality.DAMAGE is beauty after failure.It is the image after heat.After moisture.After the wrong codec.After the cracked lens.After the dirty scanner.After the tape dragged its own guts across the head.After the codec guessed wrong.After the signal forgot.After the medium left fingerprints on what it was trying to show.DAMAGE is not a filter.It is a visible history of injury, translation, contamination, and persistence.An undamaged image pretends to be pure.A damaged image admits that it traveled.That travel leaves marks.Every medium leaves a signature:- tape smears- phosphors bloom- grain swarms- codecs chunk- glass fogs- scanners band- channels misalign- frames echo- colors rot- edges tear- ghosts remainThese are not mistakes to erase.They are evidence.DAMAGE rejects fake generic "brokenness."It rejects clean nostalgia with no failure logic.It rejects the flattening of all broken media into one mushy aesthetic.A VHS tracking error is not a JPEG block field.Halation is not bloom.Lens grease is not signal bleed.Grain is not sensor noise.A datamosh is not a scratched negative.A broken thing is not just "fucked up."The medium matters. The cause matters. The scar has structure.DAMAGE is interested in images that have been touched by time.By time.By matter.By compression.By friction.By error correction.By dirt.By electricity.By chemistry.By human stupidity.By machine fatigue.It is not enough for an image to look "fucked up." It should look fucked up in a way that tells a story about systems.DAMAGE therefore insists on:- medium specificity- causal logic- stack awareness- severity control- perceptual consequence- translation into usable visual languageDAMAGE is not nostalgic.It is not cynical.It is not nostalgic for one era over another.It is interested in all damaged seeing:- analog- digital- optical- chemical- magnetic- streamed- scanned- copied- replayed- reheated- resaved- misread- partially decoded- beautifully ruinedDAMAGE is where the image stops pretending it was ever pure.
