# CHEMICAL DAMAGEThis file documenting damage from chemical interaction—processing errors, environmental chemistry, and deliberate or accidental chemical exposure.## DefinitionChemical damage refers to visible alteration from chemical processes: development errors, fixer contamination, environmental pollutants, and reactive degradation.## Categories### Processing errors#### Development issues- Under/over development- Developer contamination- Uneven development (agitation failure)- Temperature errors#### Fixer problems- Insufficient fixing- Fixer exhaustion- Contamination causing stains#### Bleach / blix errors- Color shift- Dye destruction- Retained silver### Environmental chemistry#### Air pollutants- Sulfur compounds (silver tarnishing)- Ozone (dye fading)- Acidic gases (paper degradation)#### Water damage- Mineral deposits- Staining- Emulsion softening or removal#### Biological secretions- Mold acids- Insect damage- Rodent urine (serious issue in archives)### Deliberate chemical exposure#### Intentional distressing- Bleaching- Dyeing / toning- Solvent application#### Vandalism / accident- Spills- Fire / smoke- Chemical storage proximity## Visual signatures- Often color-specific changes- Pattern related to chemical contact (flow marks, pooling)- Progressive or sudden depending on exposure- May affect specific layers (emulsion vs base)## Common media### Film- Staining from poor fixing- Dye shift from bad processing- Vinegar syndrome (chemical breakdown)### Prints- Yellowing from residual chemistry- Foxing from acid migration- Silver mirroring from oxidation### Tape- Binder hydrolysis (chemical breakdown)- Lubricant degradation## Severity progressionS1–S2: subtle color shift, minor stainingS3: obvious chemical alteration, pattern visibleS4–S5: severe degradation, information destruction, medium failure## Stack partnersOften with:- heat/humidity (accelerates chemical damage)- storage damage- handling contamination## Prompting notesUseful:- poorly fixed film staining- chemical splash damage- processing error color shift- air pollutant tarnishing## SummaryChemical damage is the molecular level of injury—bonds breaking, dyes shifting, emulsions dissolving as chemistry rewrites the image's material foundation.
