# OPTICAL INTERFERENCE METHODSThis file documents deliberate and accidental optical manipulation—ways that light paths are altered to create or simulate damage effects.## DefinitionOptical interference methods refer to techniques that modify, obstruct, or manipulate light paths to create visible image effects, ranging from deliberate aesthetic choices to accidental contamination.## Categories### Deliberate optical manipulation#### Diffusion filters- Engineered softening- Controlled bloom- Uniform or patterned#### Lens attachments- Vaseline/petroleum (grease diffusion)- Nylon stocking (mesh diffusion)- Prismatic effects- Split-field diopters#### Camera modifications- Lensbaby / tilt-shift- Pinhole conversion- Damaged lens elements (deliberate)#### In-camera effects- Lens whacking (partial detachment)- Freelensing (lens removed from mount)- Filter stacking### Accidental optical interference#### Contamination (covered in detail elsewhere)- Dust, moisture, oils on optics- Condensation- Damage to coatings#### Mechanical issues- Loose elements- Decentered optics- Fungus etching#### Environmental- Atmospheric haze- Heat shimmer- Window glass interference## Visual signatures- Often affect focus, contrast, and highlight behavior- May create specific patterns (mesh, streak, radial)- Usually affect entire image or specific zones- Often highlight-dependent## Common methods and their effects| Method | Primary effect | Secondary effects ||--------|---------------|-------------------|| Vaseline | Diffusion, glow | Irregular, tactile || Nylon mesh | Softening, pattern | Moiré potential || Lens whacking | Tilt/shift, flare | Light leaks, instability || Condensation | General haze | Droplet patterns || Damaged coating | Flare, ghosting | Color shift, contrast loss |## Severity progressionS1–S2: subtle diffusion, slight softeningS3: obvious optical manipulation, clear effectS4–S5: severe obstruction, near-abstraction, functional impairment## Stack partnersOften with:- flare and bloom- focus effects- overexposure- specific lighting conditions (backlight essential for many effects)## Prompting notesUseful:- Vaseline-smeared lens diffusion- lens whacking tilt-shift- nylon stocking soft focus- condensation-hazed optics## SummaryOptical interference methods are the deliberate or accidental sabotage of clean optical paths—turning lenses from precision instruments into impressionistic paintbrushes.
