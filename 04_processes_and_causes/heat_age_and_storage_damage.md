# HEAT, AGE, AND STORAGE DAMAGEThis file documents damage from environmental exposure—thermal stress, time-based degradation, and poor storage conditions.## DefinitionHeat, age, and storage damage refers to progressive material degradation from environmental factors: temperature, humidity, light exposure, and time itself.## Categories### Heat damage#### Thermal warping- Dimensional distortion from heat- Curling, buckling, delamination#### Chemical acceleration- Faster emulsion degradation- Binder breakdown (sticky shed)- Color dye shift#### Physical melting / deformation- Tape base deformation- Optical disc warping- Plastic component failure### Age-related degradation#### Chemical breakdown- Dye fading (chromogenic materials)- Silver oxidation (black and white)- Binder hydrolysis (tape)- Plasticizer migration#### Mechanical fatigue- Embrittlement- Loss of flexibility- Internal stress cracking### Storage damage#### Humidity / moisture- Mold growth- Emulsion softening- Paper buckling (cockling)- Metal corrosion#### Light exposure- Fading- Color shift- Highlight detail loss#### Air quality- Oxidation- Acidic degradation- Pollutant staining## Visual signatures- Gradual, progressive change- Often affects entire collection similarly- Pattern related to storage location (edge fading, etc.)- Synergistic effects (heat + humidity worse than either alone)## Common media### Film- Vinegar syndrome (acetate base)- Color dye fade (magenta shift common)- Emulsion cracking### Magnetic tape- Sticky shed syndrome- Print-through- Base warping### Prints / paper- Yellowing- Foxing (rust spots)- Brittleness### Optical discs- Delamination- Oxidation of reflective layer- "Disc rot"## Severity progressionS1–S2: subtle color shift, slight warpingS3: obvious degradation, handling issuesS4–S5: severe information loss, medium destruction## Stack partnersOften with:- handling damage (from degraded material)- contamination (mold, dust)- chemical damage## Prompting notesUseful:- heat-warped videotape- vinegar syndrome film degradation- light-faded print colors- humidity-damaged emulsion## SummaryHeat, age, and storage damage is time and environment writing their history on the medium—the slow violence of entropy made visible.
