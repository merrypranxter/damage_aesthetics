# CAUSE-BASED PROMPTINGThis file documents how to prompt damage by describing the causal process—invoking artifacts through their origins rather than just their appearance.## DefinitionCause-based prompting specifies the mechanism, condition, or history that produces damage, allowing generative systems to infer appropriate artifacts.## Why cause-based prompting works### Process coherenceDescribing how damage happens ensures artifacts make sense together.### Medium specificityCauses imply specific media and their native behaviors.### Severity controlCause severity naturally maps to artifact severity.### Novel combinationsUnderstanding causes enables inventive but grounded hybrid stacks.## Cause categories for prompting### Physical handling> "film stored in humid basement for 30 years"> "VHS tape wound and rewound hundreds of times"> "lens wiped with shirt, leaving grease and scratches"Artifacts implied: mold, warping, scratches, contamination, wear patterns### Environmental exposure> "left in direct sunlight for years"> "exposed to cigarette smoke and cooking grease"> "water damage from flooded storage"Artifacts implied: fading, staining, chemical shift, physical damage### Technical limitation> "recorded on consumer-grade VHS at 6-hour speed"> "compressed to 64kbps for dial-up streaming"> "shot on phone in near-total darkness"Artifacts implied: bandwidth artifacts, noise, compression, sensor stress### Mechanical failure> "tracking misaligned on worn playback heads"> "projector gate damaged, scratching film"> "buffer underrun during live stream"Artifacts implied: tearing, scratches, stuttering, sync loss### Generational transfer> "16mm film → VHS copy → digital capture → YouTube upload"> "photocopy of photocopy of fax"Artifacts implied: cumulative loss, format-specific artifacts, resolution reduction## Cause-to-artifact mapping| Cause | Primary Artifacts | Secondary Artifacts ||-------|-------------------|---------------------|| Tape wear | Softness, tracking error | Chroma bleed, noise || Heat damage | Color shift, warping | Contrast loss, emulsion damage || Low bitrate | Macroblocking, banding | Mosquito noise, smear || Lens contamination | Haze, flare, bloom | Contrast loss, soft focus || Poor scanning | Banding, moiré, softness | Color shift, resolution loss |## Prompt structure: cause-based### Simple cause> "[medium] damaged by [cause], showing [severity] [artifacts]"Example: "VHS tape damaged by repeated playback on worn heads, showing moderate tracking instability and chroma bleed"### Complex cause chain> "[original state] subjected to [cause 1], resulting in [state 1], then [cause 2], showing [final artifacts]"Example: "16mm film subjected to humid storage, resulting in slight warp and mold spots, then transferred to VHS with tracking errors, showing cumulative softness and instability"### Environmental cause> "[medium] exposed to [environmental conditions] for [duration], showing [degradation]"Example: "Color print exposed to direct sunlight and cigarette smoke for 20 years, showing severe magenta fade and yellow staining"## Cause severity language### Mild cause- "slightly mishandled"- "briefly exposed to"- "minor wear from"- "early stage of"### Moderate cause- "well-used"- "stored in"- "transferred using"- "noticeable wear from"### Severe cause- "severely neglected"- "extensively damaged by"- "catastrophically failed"- "advanced deterioration from"## Cause-based vs appearance-based### Appearance-based (weaker)> "glitchy VHS with tracking lines and noise"### Cause-based (stronger)> "VHS tape played on misaligned deck with dirty heads, showing tracking error bands and analog noise from signal weakness"Cause-based prompts:- Explain why artifacts appear- Suggest coherent combinations- Enable severity scaling- Ground results in believable process## Common cause-based prompts by medium### Film> "35mm negative processed in exhausted developer, showing uneven development and reduced contrast"> "Film stored in non-archival sleeve, showing vinegar syndrome and emulsion buckling"### Video> "Betacam master dubbed to consumer VHS at 6-hour speed, showing severe chroma noise and softness"> "Analog broadcast recorded off-air with weak signal, showing snow and sync instability"### Digital> "RAW image aggressively compressed for web, showing 8×8 JPEG blocks and color subsampling"> "4K video streamed at 1Mbps, showing severe macroblocking and temporal smear"### Print> "Laser print from nearly empty toner cartridge, showing streaking and dropout"> "Document photocopied 10 times, showing contrast flattening and misregistration accumulation"## Testing cause-based promptsEvaluate by asking:- Does the cause imply the artifacts described?- Could this cause produce other artifacts too?- Is the severity appropriate to the cause?- Would an expert find the combination plausible?## SummaryCause-based prompting is forensic imagination—describing the history of injury that produces visible scars.
