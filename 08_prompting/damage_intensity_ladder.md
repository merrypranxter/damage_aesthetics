# DAMAGE INTENSITY LADDERThis file provides granular severity language for precise damage control in prompts.## DefinitionThe damage intensity ladder is a structured vocabulary for specifying exact severity levels across all damage types.## The ladder structure### Level 0: Absent> "clean," "pristine," "undamaged," "reference quality"### Level 1: Trace> "barely perceptible," "subtle suggestion of," "faint hint," "archaeological whisper"Visible only on close inspection or knowing comparison.### Level 2: Mild> "light," "gentle," "restrained," "atmospheric," "present but not dominant"Clearly visible but not compositionally active.### Level 3: Moderate> "obvious," "pronounced," "active participant," "stylistically significant"Damage shapes perception and composition.### Level 4: Severe> "heavy," "aggressive," "dominant," "compositionally defining," "threatening legibility"Damage becomes primary visual force.### Level 5: Catastrophic> "total," "collapse," "destruction," "unreadable," "signal death"Image transformed or destroyed by damage.## Intensity by artifact family### Noise/grain| Level | Language | Visual ||-------|----------|--------|| 1 | "barely perceptible grain" | Smooth with slight texture || 2 | "light film grain" | Clear but restrained texture || 3 | "obvious photochemical grain" | Texturally dominant || 4 | "heavy, coarse grain" | Detail obscured by texture || 5 | "extreme enlarged grain" | Image becoming texture field |### Smear/bleed| Level | Language | Visual ||-------|----------|--------|| 1 | "faint edge softness" | Slight spread || 2 | "light chroma drift" | Color slightly unfocused || 3 | "obvious color bleed" | Clear spreading || 4 | "severe smear" | Major information spread || 5 | "total dissolution" | Image becoming color field |### "slight compression softness" | Subtle block edges || 2 | "visible JPEG artifacts" | Clear block structure || 3 | "obvious macroblocking" | Grid dominates || 4 | "severe codec breakdown" | Major information loss || 5 | "total abstraction" | Image becomes block pattern |## Combining intensities### Uniform intensity> "moderate severity throughout"### Gradient intensity> "trace in highlights, severe in shadows"### Selective intensity> "severe damage concentrated in lower third, clean elsewhere"### Progressive intensity> "starting clean, degrading to catastrophic by frame end"## Intensity modifiers### Temporal- "increasing"- "decreasing"- "fluctuating"- "intermittent"### Spatial- "concentrated"- "diffuse"- "edge-biased"- "center-weighted"### Qualitative- "smooth"- "harsh"- "organic"- "mechanical"## Intensity in stack promptsSpecify per layer:> "VHS base at S2, tracking error at S4, CRT display at S2"Or cumulative:> "accumulating to severe through generational loss"## Avoiding intensity pitfalls### InflationUsing "extreme" for everything dilutes meaning.### InconsistencySevere damage with no cause or progression.### Vagueness"Lots of damage" without specificity.## SummaryThe intensity ladder is the volume control for damage—precise calibration from whisper to scream.
