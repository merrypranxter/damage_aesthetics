# STACK-BASED PROMPTINGThis file documents how to prompt coherent damage stacks—layered combinations of artifacts with logical relationships.## DefinitionStack-based prompting builds damage descriptions as layered combinations, specifying how artifacts coexist and interact.## Stack structure for prompts### Base layerThe foundational medium and its native state.### Native artifactsArtifacts that naturally belong to the base medium.### Cause modifiersWhat has happened to introduce additional damage.### Chain additionsSubsequent transfers, captures, or processes.### Final stateThe cumulative appearance and severity.## Prompt template> "[base medium] with [native artifacts] at [severity], [cause modifier] adding [additional artifacts], [chain step] introducing [further artifacts], resulting in [final description]"## Example stacks### Native stack> "VHS with natural chroma bleed and luma softness at moderate severity, played on slightly misaligned deck adding tracking instability, resulting in authentic consumer tape aesthetic"### Chain stack> "16mm film with fine grain and subtle gate weave, scanned to VHS in 1980s adding chroma bleed and generation loss, played on CRT with phosphor bloom, photographed with phone camera adding rolling shutter and noise reduction smear, uploaded to Instagram with recompression banding"### Hybrid stack> "Digital video with clean base, deliberately processed with film grain overlay and added gate weave for vintage effect, then compressed aggressively adding authentic macroblocking and banding, resulting in stylized hybrid with real and simulated damage"### Severe stack> "VHS original with base softness, subjected to multiple generations of copying adding noise and tracking errors, played on failing deck with severe sync loss and head clog, resulting in near-total signal collapse with occasional recognizable forms"## Stack coherence rules### Medium compatibilityCompatible: Film → VHS → digital captureQuestionable: Clean digital → VHS tracking errors (no VHS in chain)Incoherent: VHS chroma bleed + modern codec artifacts with no explanation### Severity progressionGenerally increase severity through chain:Original (S1) → First gen (S2) → Second gen (S3) → etc.Or specify preservation vs degradation at each step.### Artifact interactionDescribe how artifacts combine:- "chroma bleed softened by phosphor bloom"- "grain masked by compression smoothing"- "tracking errors emphasized by sharpening"## Stack shorthand### Arrow notation> "16mm → VHS → CRT → phone → Instagram"Implies standard artifact accumulation.### Plus notation> "VHS + CRT + minor dust"Implies native stack plus contamination.### Severity notation> "VHS[S3] + CRT[S2] + dust[S1]"Explicit severity per layer.## Common stack patterns### Archive rescue> "Damaged film [heavy scratches, mold] → careful scanning [moiré, slight softness] → digital cleanup [noise reduction artifacts]"### Network journey> "Original video → platform A [compression A] → download → platform B [compression B] → screen recording [capture artifacts]"### Time capsule> "1980s home video [VHS] → stored in attic [heat damage] → digitized [scanner artifacts] → shared online [recompression]"## Stack-based vs single-artifact### Single-artifact (limited)> "VHS tracking error"### Stack-based (rich)> "VHS tape with base softness and chroma bleed, tracking instability from worn transport, played on CRT with phosphor bloom, photographed off-screen with slight moiré"Stack-based prompts enable:- Complex, believable damage- Historical narrative- Severity control through accumulation- Richer aesthetic results## Testing stack promptsEvaluate by:- Can the stack be diagrammed as a flow?- Does each step add plausible artifacts?- Do artifacts interact logically?- Is the final severity appropriate to the chain?## SummaryStack-based prompting is building damage as architecture—each layer supporting and modifying the others to create coherent, historically grounded degradation.
