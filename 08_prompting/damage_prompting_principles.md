# DAMAGE PROMPTING PRINCIPLESThis file documents core principles for describing damage in generative prompts—how to specify artifacts clearly and effectively.## DefinitionDamage prompting principles are guidelines for translating DAMAGE taxonomy into effective language for image and video generation systems.## Core principles### 1. Specificity over vibeWeak: "glitchy, damaged look"Strong: "VHS chroma bleed with horizontal tracking instability and analog noise field"Specify:- Medium- Artifact type- Behavior- Severity### 2. Cause and mechanismInclude how the damage happens:- "from magnetic tape wear"- "caused by low-bitrate compression"- "due to lens contamination"This grounds the effect in believable process.### 3. Severity languageUse clear severity indicators:- Trace/subtle/faint (S1)- Moderate/visible/obvious (S2–S3)- Severe/aggressive/catastrophic (S4–S5)### 4. Stack logicBuild coherent combinations:- Native: "VHS softness with chroma bleed and tracking noise"- Chain: "film scanned to VHS, played on CRT, filmed with phone"- Hybrid: "film grain with digital compression artifacts"### 5. Placement and compositionSpecify where damage appears:- "light leak from top edge"- "scratches across lower third"- "noise concentrated in shadows"### 6. Temporal language (for video)Specify behavior over time:- "crawling noise field"- "unstable tracking with horizontal jitter"- "frame-to-frame shimmer"## Prompt structure templates### Single artifact> "[medium] [artifact] with [severity], [behavior], [cause context]"Example: "VHS chroma bleed with moderate severity, horizontal color smearing on saturated edges, from analog composite signal bandwidth limitation"### Stack> "[base medium] with [native artifacts], [severity], plus [additional artifacts] from [cause]"Example: "16mm film base with fine grain and subtle gate weave, plus heavy dust and hair contamination from archival neglect, plus scanner banding from flatbed digitization"### Chain> "[original] → [transfer 1] → [transfer 2] → [final state]"Example: "Original 35mm film → 1980s VHS duplication → CRT playback → phone camera capture → social media upload with recompression"## Language to avoid### Vague trend words- "glitchy"- "retro"- "vintage"- "aesthetic"- "vibes"### Generic damage- "damaged"- "broken"- "corrupted"- "distorted"### Medium-mashing- "VHS film grain" (VHS doesn't have grain)- "digital light leaks" (light leaks are optical/analog)### Severity inflation- "extreme" without specification- "heavy" without behavior description## Effective damage vocabulary### MediumsVHS, Betamax, 8mm film, 16mm film, 35mm film, CRT, LCD, CCD sensor, CMOS sensor, JPEG, H.264, laser print, xerox### Artifactschroma bleed, tracking error, macroblocking, film grain, phosphor bloom, gate weave, head-switching noise, quantization banding, moiré, lens flare, halation### Behaviorssmearing, crawling, jittering, tearing, blooming, crawling, shimmering, drifting### Severitiesfaint, subtle, light, moderate, pronounced, obvious, heavy, severe, aggressive, catastrophic## Testing promptsEvaluate prompts by:- Can a human expert identify the medium?- Are artifacts causally coherent?- Is severity appropriate to description?- Would the result be distinguishable from generic "glitch"?## SummaryGood damage prompting is technical description with aesthetic intent—specific enough to generate believable artifacts, structured enough to maintain coherence.
