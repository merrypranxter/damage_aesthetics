# ANTI-GENERIC DAMAGE PROMPTINGThis file documents how to avoid the most common failures in damage prompting—vague, flattened, or incorrect descriptions.## The generic damage problemGeneric prompts produce generic results:- "glitchy"- "damaged"- "retro effect"- "VHS style"These collapse rich taxonomy into mush.## Common generic failures### 1. The "glitch" catchallWeak: "digital glitch effect"Better: "H.264 macroblocking with temporal prediction error"Best: "low-bitrate streaming artifact showing 16×16 block segmentation and motion-compensated smear"### 2. Retro without specificityWeak: "retro film look"Better: "16mm film with photochemical grain"Best: "Kodachrome 40 with fine grain, slight gate weave, and characteristic color saturation"### 3. VHS stereotypesWeak: "VHS effect with scanlines"Better: "VHS with chroma bleed and tracking instability"Best: "consumer VHS at 6-hour speed showing composite chroma noise, head-switching artifacts, and horizontal jitter from capstan wear"### 4. Medium mixing without logicWrong: "film grain on digital video with VHS tracking"Right: "digital video with film grain overlay, separately: VHS transfer showing chroma bleed"Or chain logically: "film → VHS → digital capture"### 5. Severity without behaviorWeak: "heavy damage"Better: "severe chroma bleed with horizontal tearing"Best: "S4 severity chroma bleed causing color information to smear 20% beyond object edges, combined with S3 tracking instability showing horizontal band displacement"## Anti-generic strategies### Name the mediumEvery damage belongs to a system. Name it.### Name the artifactUse specific taxonomy terms: chroma bleed, not "color weirdness."### Name the causeHow did this happen? Mechanism grounds the effect.### Name the severityQuantify or qualify: moderate, severe, trace.### Describe the behaviorWhat does it do? Smear, crawl, jitter, bloom?## Generic phrase blacklistAvoid as primary description:- glitchy- damaged- corrupted- retro- vintage- old school- lo-fi- aesthetic- vibes- style- effect- filter- lookThese can modify specific terms:- "glitchy macroblocking" (acceptable)- "glitchy video" (unacceptable)## Redemption: specific + atmosphericCombine precision with mood:> "VHS chroma bleed and tracking instability (specific) creating melancholic domestic nostalgia (atmospheric)"Specificity enables the atmospheric effect.## Testing for genericismAsk:- Could this describe multiple unrelated media?- Would an expert know what I mean?- Does this prompt generate unique results?- Have I used any blacklist words as primary description?If yes to any: revise for specificity.## Examples: generic to specific| Generic | Specific ||---------|----------|| "glitch art" | "datamosh-style interframe prediction residue with block migration" || "film look" | "35mm Kodak Vision3 with fine grain, slight halation, and photochemical color response" || "VHS horror" | "VHS generation loss with tracking error, chroma noise, and unstable playback lock" || "digital decay" | "JPEG macroblocking with generation loss, screenshot recompression, and platform transcode artifacts" || "damaged photo" | "color print with light fade, handling creases, and emulsion cracking from vinegar syndrome" |## SummaryAnti-generic prompting is the discipline of specificity—refusing the convenience of vague trend language for the precision of technical description.
